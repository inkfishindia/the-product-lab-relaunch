### 1.1 Photography Setup (One-Time, Pre-Production)

**Minimum required setup (zero studio budget):**

- **Surfaces:** 3 options
  1. Black matte posterboard (₹50 for pack of 5) — smooth, folds flat, portable
  2. Dark concrete floor area (free) — use natural rough texture
  3. Dark fabric (charcoal or black linen, ₹200–400 for 2m × 1m piece) — drapes nicely, easy to refold

- **Lighting:** Single directional source
  - Option 1: iPhone flashlight (standard on all phones)
  - Option 2: Desk lamp with 40W warm LED bulb (₹200–300 one-time)
  - Option 3: Phone camera + natural window light (free, but time-dependent)
  - **Recommended:** Desk lamp + posterboard (best control, repeatable)

- **Phone camera settings (no special equipment):**
  - iPhone: use native Camera app, Portrait mode off (too much blur), Macro mode on if available
  - Android: use native Camera app, manual focus on product, auto-white-balance
  - **Don't use:** filters, beauty modes, HDR (all distort colors)
  - **Shoot format:** landscape (easier framing for product grid)
  - **Resolution:** shoot at max available (4K if device supports; downscale later)

- **Lighting technique (test on 1–2 products first):**
  - Position light source at 45° angle from product (upper left or right)
  - Shadow should fall away from camera, creating definition
  - Product should be brightest element in frame
  - No light reflections on product (adjust angle if glare appears)
  - Test shot: take 3 angles of same product, pick best

- **Post-processing (free tools):**
  - Crop: use Canva or native phone crop tool
  - Color: minimal adjustment in Canva (don't oversaturate)
  - No filters, no blur effects, no watermarks
  - Export as JPEG at 80% quality (smaller files, acceptable quality)

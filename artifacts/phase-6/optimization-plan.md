## Part 1: 30-Day Performance Review Framework

At Day 30 of launch, Dan sits down and reviews the first month. This is the decision point for what happens in Month 2 and beyond.

### Core Metrics Review

These are the five metrics that matter most. Everything else supports these five.

**Metric 1: Conversion Rate**
- **Definition:** (Total orders / Total sessions) × 100
- **Target:** 2-3% by Day 30
- **What it means:** Of every 100 people who visit the site, 2-3 buy something. This is a healthy early-stage conversion rate.
- **Below target (< 1.5%):** Something is broken. Either the traffic quality is wrong, the checkout is confusing, or the product descriptions don't match what people expect.
- **Above target (> 3%):** You have product-market fit signals. Consider increasing marketing investment.

**Metric 2: Average Order Value (AOV)**
- **Definition:** Total revenue / Total orders
- **Target:** ₹350-400 by Day 30
- **What it means:** Customers are bundling products or trying multiple items. This is necessary for unit economics (see Phase 1 baseline-financials.md).
- **Below target (< ₹300):** Customers are buying single items (likely card stickers). You need better bundle recommendations or upselling on product pages.
- **Above target (> ₹450):** Your bundles are working. Consider promoting them more.

**Metric 3: Repeat Purchase Rate**
- **Definition:** (Customers who purchased twice / Total customers) × 100
- **Target:** 5-10% by Day 30 (maybe 2-3 repeat customers if <50 total customers)
- **What it means:** People are coming back. This is retention signal.
- **Below target (0%):** Do not panic at Day 30 — this takes time. But by Day 60, you should see 2-3 repeat customers. If not, retention email sequence needs work.
- **Above target (> 10%):** Your email or WhatsApp flow is working. Lean into re-engagement.

**Metric 4: Product Mix**
- **Definition:** Which products are selling? What % of orders include each hero product?
- **Target:** Card stickers >40% of orders, attitude keychains >30%, pins >20%
- **What it means:** Hero products are behaving as expected. If not, the product positioning or photography may be off.
- **Variation:** If one product is >70% of orders, you may have overemphasized it. Test de-emphasizing and see if product mix balances.

**Metric 5: Traffic Source Mix**
- **Definition:** Where are customers coming from? (Instagram, email, WhatsApp, organic, direct, other)
- **Target:** Instagram >50%, Email >20%, WhatsApp >15%, organic >10%
- **What it means:** Instagram is the primary discovery channel (as planned). Email and WhatsApp drive repeat traffic.
- **Variation:** If organic is >15%, your SEO or word-of-mouth is strong. If <5%, you may need to improve site discoverability.

### Secondary Metrics to Review

**Email metrics:**
- Welcome email open rate (target: >25%)
- Email-to-purchase conversion (target: 5-10% of email clicks → purchase)
- Abandoned cart recovery rate (target: 10-15% of abandoned carts recovered)

**WhatsApp metrics:**
- WhatsApp opt-in growth rate (target: 20+ new subscribers/week)
- Click-through rate on broadcast (target: 20-30% of subscribers click links)

**Instagram metrics:**
- Follower growth rate (target: 100-150 followers by Day 30)
- Reel views (target: 200-500 views per launch-day Reel)
- Save rate (target: 5-10% of viewers save the post)

**Customer service metrics:**
- Response time to DMs (target: <2 hours)
- Resolution rate on complaints (target: 100% resolved within 7 days)
- CSAT on support (target: >80% of customers report satisfaction)

---

### What to Do If Metrics Miss Targets

**If conversion rate is <1.5%:**

Action: Audit the checkout funnel.

1. **Check GA4:** Where are people dropping off?
   - If many people view product pages but don't add to cart → product description problem
   - If many add to cart but don't reach checkout → cart page problem
   - If many reach checkout but don't complete → payment method or trust issue

2. **Check Microsoft Clarity:** Watch session recordings to see where users are struggling.

3. **Run quick fixes:**
   - Add testimonials or social proof to product pages
   - Simplify checkout (remove unnecessary fields)
   - Add payment method icons (Razorpay supports UPI, cards, wallets — show these clearly)
   - Test a "trust badge" or security message near payment button

4. **If still below target by Day 45:** Consider paid traffic to test messaging. A small ₹5,000 ad spend can tell you if the problem is traffic quality vs. site problem.

---

**If AOV is <₹300:**

Action: Increase bundling and upselling.

1. **Check product pages:** Do they include "often bought together" recommendations?
2. **Check cart page:** Is there a "add one more item and get free shipping" message?
3. **Check checkout:** Is there an upsell opportunity at the last moment? (e.g., "add a pin for ₹99 more"?)
4. **Test a bundle discount:** For Day 31-45, offer "Buy any 2 products, 15% off." Track if this increases AOV.

---

**If repeat purchase rate is 0%:**

Action: Check your retention emails.

1. **Review email sequence:** Did the Day 7 post-purchase email go out? Did it ask for feedback?
2. **Check email open rates:** If post-purchase email open rate is <15%, the subject line or send time may be wrong.
3. **Check WhatsApp broadcast:** Are you sending drop announcements to existing customers? Make them feel like VIPs.
4. **Add a referral incentive:** "Share your order on WhatsApp, get 10% off your next purchase." Test this in Week 2 of Month 2.

---

**If a product is underperforming (<10% of orders):**

Action: Diagnose and test.

1. **Check photography:** Is the product image compelling? Does it show the product in context?
2. **Check copy:** Does the product description follow copy-system.md? Does it name the person it is for?
3. **Check positioning:** Is the product placed on the homepage and in relevant collections?
4. **Test repositioning:** Move it to a more prominent location for one week and measure change in add-to-cart.
5. **If still <10%:** The product may not be right for the audience. Consider retiring it or redesigning it.

## Part 3: A/B Test Roadmap (Month 2+)

Once you have 30 days of baseline data, you can start A/B testing. Here are the first 5 tests to run.

### Test 1: Product Page Headline

**Current state:** "For the person who has already written off small talk as a waste of time" (example from copy-system.md)

**Hypothesis:** A shorter, more direct headline will increase add-to-cart rate.

**Test:** Create an alternate headline: "For people tired of small talk."

**Run:** Show version A (current) to 50% of traffic, version B (new) to 50% for 2 weeks.

**Success metric:** Version B has >10% higher add-to-cart rate than version A.

**Platform:** Shopify/Fynd native A/B testing or use GA4 segments.

---

### Test 2: Product Page Photos

**Current state:** Flat product shot on white background.

**Hypothesis:** Lifestyle photo (product in context) will increase add-to-cart rate by showing how the product is used.

**Test:** Add a lifestyle photo as the primary image on 5 underperforming products. Keep the flat shot as secondary.

**Run:** For 2 weeks, measure add-to-cart rate on these 5 products vs. products with only flat shots.

**Success metric:** Products with lifestyle photo have >15% higher add-to-cart rate.

**Platform:** Manually track, or use Clarity to compare engagement on different products.

---

### Test 3: Email Send Time

**Current state:** All emails sent at 9:00 AM.

**Hypothesis:** Evening send (6:00 PM) may have higher open rate due to leisure browsing time.

**Test:** Split email list in half. Send identical email to Group A at 9:00 AM, Group B at 6:00 PM.

**Run:** For 1 week, track open rate and click rate for both groups.

**Success metric:** 6:00 PM email has >5% higher open rate.

**Platform:** Klaviyo automation (can split at send time).

---

### Test 4: Discount Code Offer

**Current state:** 10% EARLYBIRD code in launch-week emails.

**Hypothesis:** 15% discount may increase conversion on abandoned carts without cannibalizing full-price orders.

**Test:** Send abandoned cart email to Group A with standard message (no discount). Group B gets same message + "use code COMEBACK15 for 15% off."

**Run:** For 2 weeks, track recovery rate and AOV for both groups.

**Success metric:** Group B has >20% higher recovery rate, even if AOV is slightly lower.

**Platform:** Klaviyo automation (can use different template per segment).

---

### Test 5: WhatsApp Broadcast Cadence

**Current state:** 1-2 broadcasts per week.

**Hypothesis:** 2-3 broadcasts per week (e.g., product feature, customer story, new drop preview) will increase repeat purchases without increasing unsubscribes.

**Test:** Increase broadcast frequency for 2 weeks. Track unsubscribe rate and purchases from WhatsApp traffic.

**Success metric:** Unsubscribe rate stays <2%, but purchases from WhatsApp increase >15%.

**Platform:** Gupshup/MSG91 analytics.

## Part 4: Merchandising Refinement

By Day 30, Dan knows which products are selling and which are not. This is the moment to refine the catalog.

### Fast-Moving Products (Restock or Feature)

If card stickers or attitude keychains are >40% of orders:
- Increase the quantity in stock (reorder from suppliers)
- Feature them more prominently on homepage
- Create a dedicated "Best Sellers" collection
- Consider new variants or colors

### Slow-Moving Products (Retire or Redesign)

If a product is <5% of orders by Day 30:
- Check the product page: is copy and photography compelling?
- Check the positioning: is it in the right collection?
- If both are strong but sales are still low: retire the product and use shelf space for something new
- Do not keep low-performing products "just in case" — they tie up capital

### Bundle Opportunities

If customers are buying single products but AOV is low:
- Create a "Starter Bundle" (card sticker + attitude keychain, ₹399)
- Create a "Collector's Bundle" (3-5 pins, ₹699-899)
- Create a "Gift Bundle" (card sticker + magnet + pin, ₹499, gift-wrapped)
- Price bundles 10-15% cheaper than buying separately (incentive to bundle)

### New Drop Planning

By Day 45, plan the next drop. Based on Day 30 learnings:
- What product type is winning? (card stickers, keychains, or pins?)
- What themes are customers buying? (attitude, K-Pop, anime, design?)
- What price point works? (₹149, ₹249, or ₹349?)

Create Drop 2 based on product-market fit signals, not assumptions.

## Part 5: Retention Improvement Plan (Month 2)

Month 1 is about acquisition. Month 2 is about retention. These tactics get repeat customers buying again.

### Tactic 1: "Share Your Order" Referral Program

**How it works:**
- Every customer who buys gets a "Share your order" message in the Day 7 email and WhatsApp
- They screenshot their order or product photo and share it on Instagram Stories, tagging @the.product.lab
- You repost their photo and give them 10% off their next purchase (single-use code, valid for 30 days)

**Expected outcome:** 10-15% of customers will share, generating user-generated content and repeat purchase intent

**Implementation:**
- Create a unique 10% code for each customer (e.g., SHARE-[Customer Name])
- Send it via email on Day 7 with a clear CTA: "Screenshot your order on Stories, tag us, and get 10% off your next purchase"
- Track which customers use the code (indicates intent to buy again)

---

### Tactic 2: "Subscribers Only" Early Access

**How it works:**
- Every 2 weeks, announce a new drop to email/WhatsApp subscribers 48 hours before public launch
- Subscribers get first pick and free shipping over ₹300 (vs. ₹499 for everyone else)
- Non-subscribers must wait 48 hours to buy, but get the same free shipping at ₹499

**Expected outcome:** Drives email/WhatsApp signups from non-buyers and encourages repeat engagement from existing subscribers

---

### Tactic 3: "Double the Loyalty" Program

**How it works:**
- First purchase: earn 1 point per rupee spent (₹100 = 1 point)
- Second purchase: earn 2 points per rupee (customers who return get accelerated rewards)
- 100 points = ₹100 discount on any order
- Loyalty status shown in order confirmation email: "You're 30 points away from ₹100 off your next order"

**Expected outcome:** Customers feel like repeat purchasing is rewarded. This is especially powerful for small-order customers.

**Implementation:** Manual tracking at first (spreadsheet). Automate with Klaviyo later if viable.

---

### Tactic 4: Monthly Email: "What You Bought & What's New"

**How it works:**
- Send one email per month to all past customers
- Show: "You bought [product], which is [theme]. Here's what else in that theme is new:"
- Personalize recommendations based on purchase history

**Expected outcome:** Reminds customers the brand exists. Personalizes re-engagement.

---

### Tactic 5: WhatsApp VIP Group

**How it works:**
- Create a WhatsApp group for top 20-30 customers (those who bought 2+ times or spent >₹500)
- Send early previews, exclusive offers, and creator stories to the VIP group before public announcement
- Make them feel special and heard

**Expected outcome:** Creates community and increases retention of best customers.

## Part 6: Community Building & Brand Culture

Month 2 is when community starts. These are low-cost, high-impact tactics.

### Tactic 1: Weekly Instagram Stories from Customers

**How it works:**
- Every week, reach out to 2-3 customers with: "Can I share a photo of your [product] in our Stories this week? You'll be featured."
- Most will say yes
- Repost their photo in Instagram Stories, tag them, thank them
- Their followers see the mention and may visit the site

**Expected outcome:** 5-10 story posts per month, generating 100-200 profile visits and ~₹2-5K in attributed revenue

---

### Tactic 2: "Artist Feature" Series

**How it works:**
- Every month, dedicate an Instagram post and email to one artist whose work you sell
- Tell their story, show their design process, link to their designs
- Tag them and ask them to share (their audience may discover the brand)

**Expected outcome:** Builds artist community. Artists become informal advocates. Creates emotional depth to the brand.

---

### Tactic 3: Monthly Newsletter (Optional, Month 3+)

**How it works:**
- Once you have solid content rhythm and 500+ email subscribers, consider a monthly newsletter
- Format: Artist feature + Product story + Customer highlight + What's coming next
- Length: ~500 words, designed to be forwarded/shared

**Expected outcome:** Deepens relationship with audience, generates >5% email-to-purchase conversion

---

### Tactic 4: Close Friends List (Instagram)

**How it works:**
- Create an Instagram "Close Friends" list of 50-100 best customers and followers
- Share exclusive content: early drop previews, behind-the-scenes, design sneak peeks (not shared on main feed)
- Keep this list feeling exclusive and VIP

**Expected outcome:** Drives FOMO for non-included followers. Increases engagement from best customers.

## Part 7: Financial Check-In (Month 1 End)

By Day 30, check the unit economics against Phase 1 baseline-financials.md.

### Break-Even Analysis

From Phase 1, we know:
- COD orders <₹200 lose money
- Target: ₹350+ AOV
- Target: 2%+ conversion rate

**If these targets are hit by Day 30:**
- Assume 100 orders in Month 1, average ₹350 each = ₹35,000 revenue
- COGS (~40% of revenue) = ₹14,000
- Shipping (Shiprocket, ~₹50/order) = ₹5,000
- Payment processing (Razorpay, ~2% of revenue) = ₹700
- Platform fee (Fynd, ~2% of revenue) = ₹700
- Marketing (Seeding product cost) = ₹23,000 (one-time, doesn't repeat)
- **Month 1 net (without seeding cost) = ₹35,000 - ₹14,000 - ₹5,000 - ₹700 - ₹700 = ₹14,600**
- **Month 1 net (with seeding) = -₹8,400 (investment in brand building)**

This is expected and healthy. Month 2 should be profitable without seeding costs.

## Part 8: Go/No-Go Decision (Day 30)

At the end of Day 30, Dan and Harley make a go/no-go decision on Month 2 investment.

### Go Signals (Proceed to Month 2 with Confidence)

✓ Conversion rate is 2%+ (target hit)
✓ AOV is ₹350+ (target hit)
✓ 100+ orders in Month 1 (strong demand signal)
✓ 0 major product/fulfillment issues (operational confidence)
✓ Card sticker pricing is validated (not getting pushback)
✓ Community engagement is real (organic shares, testimonials)

**Next step:** Month 2 optimization plan, increased marketing investment, consider paid ads.

---

### Caution Signals (Investigate, May Extend Month 1)

⚠ Conversion rate is 1-2% (below target, but salvageable)
⚠ AOV is ₹300-350 (slightly below, but trending up)
⚠ 50-100 orders in Month 1 (lower demand, but not zero)
⚠ Product issues with fulfillment or returns (fix ops before scaling)
⚠ Low email/WhatsApp engagement (retention may be weak)

**Next step:** Debug specific issues, run optimization tests, extend runway to Day 45 before scaling.

---

### No-Go Signals (Pivot or Pause)

✗ Conversion rate is <1% (major messaging or product problem)
✗ AOV is <₹300 (customer not seeing value)
✗ <30 orders in Month 1 (no meaningful demand signal)
✗ High return/complaint rate (product or fulfillment issues)
✗ No organic engagement (community not resonating with brand voice)

**Next step:** Pause external marketing. Conduct customer research (interview past buyers). Identify root cause. Consider rebrand or repositioning before scaling.

## Core idea

The coming soon page should not behave like a waiting room. It should act like the first expression of the brand.

The recommended concept is:

**Pick Your Opinion**

Visitors choose the version of TPL that feels most like them, see a tailored product-and-copy preview, then join the first-access list from inside that chosen lane.

This makes the page do four jobs at once:

1. Reinforce positioning: TPL is about identity, not generic accessories.
2. Create participation: the visitor makes a choice instead of passively reading.
3. Improve capture quality: the signup happens after self-identification.
4. Support the real funnel: the page generates language that is easy to forward on WhatsApp.

---

## Why this concept fits the strategy

### From positioning

- The brand territory is "small objects with big opinions."
- The emotional job is "let me be specific about who I am without having to explain it."
- A selector-driven page lets the visitor perform that exact behavior on first contact.

### From customer insight

- Discovery happens on Instagram.
- Validation happens in the inner circle, usually on WhatsApp.
- A stronger pre-launch page should create something worth sending to a friend, not just something worth filling out.

### From launch strategy

- The teaser arc moves mystery → positioning → reveal → anticipation.
- This page should sit between positioning and reveal.
- It needs enough interaction to hold attention, but still convert quickly on mobile.

## Interaction model

### Step 1: Hero statement

Open with a strong headline and a clear promise:

- The relaunch is sharper
- The page is for early access
- The visitor is about to pick a lane

### Step 2: Opinion picker

Offer 4 selectable tones:

- No Filter
- Soft Chaos
- Quiet Flex
- Gift Scout

These are not navigation filters. They are identity prompts.

### Step 3: Tailored preview

Each selected tone updates:

- headline
- short paragraph
- hero product mix
- AOV/bundle angle
- share line

### Step 4: Capture

Collect:

- email
- optional WhatsApp number
- stored selected tone

This lets the launch list become more useful later for copy segmentation.

### Step 5: Forward prompt

Provide one short share line per tone.

This is crucial because the likely user behavior is:

Instagram → page → WhatsApp share → friend validation → signup or purchase later

## Experience principles

### 1. The form is not the hero

The old page asks for signup too early. The new page earns the signup by giving the visitor something to do first.

### 2. The copy stays opinionated

Avoid soft ecommerce language. The page should sound like the brand, not like software.

### 3. The visual direction stays Darkroom

- dark background
- dramatic accent punctures in red and yellow
- Barlow Condensed doing the heavy lifting
- product previews framed like editorial chips, not marketplace cards

### 4. Every section should support mobile use

The page must work as a single-scroll phone experience:

- big type
- clear thumb targets
- fast state changes
- no heavy image dependency needed for the concept to land

---

## Content architecture

1. Ticker / energy strip
2. Hero statement
3. Opinion picker
4. Dynamic preview panel
5. Waitlist form
6. Share prompt
7. Why-this-works explanation
8. Website direction teaser

This sequence mirrors the broader launch strategy and makes the page feel connected to the full relaunch ecosystem.

---

## Success criteria

The page is doing its job if it:

1. Feels unmistakably like TPL within the first screen.
2. Gives users a reason to interact before asking for a signup.
3. Produces copy that feels natural to forward on WhatsApp.
4. Introduces the future site tone and structure, not just the launch list.
5. Makes the card sticker feel like the hero without turning the rest of the brand into background noise.

# Visual Identity — Rebrand (Light + Bold System)
**Phase:** 4
**Producing Agent:** Sean
**Date:** 2026-03-27
**Status:** draft
**Reviewer:** Harley

---

## Why This Changed

The Darkroom system (#1A1A1A base) pulled the brand toward premium and editorial. Dark backgrounds in the Indian consumer market read luxury — Tanishq, Zara, high-end. TPL sells ₹149-249 opinion stickers. The visual system was creating a disconnect between what the brand looked like and what it actually was.

This document supersedes D-015 and establishes the Light + Bold system.

What didn't change: the typography, the accent colors, the brand territory, the voice. Those were always right. Only the base flips.

---

## The Brand in One Line (for every visual decision)

**Record store soul. Candy store body.**

Soul = opinionated, curated, specific point of view. You either get it or you don't.
Body = abundant, colourful, fun. Everything visible. Pick it up. Want more than one.

Every visual decision should pass this test: does it feel curated and confident (record store) while also feeling abundant and fun (candy store)? If it leans too precious or minimal, it's drifted. If it leans too chaotic or generic, it's drifted.

## Colour System

### Base Palette

| Name | Hex | Use |
|------|-----|-----|
| Paper | #F5F0EB | Primary background — all pages, all surfaces |
| Ink | #1A1A1A | Primary type — all body copy, headings, product names |
| Loud | #E63B2E | CTAs, price treatments, sale badges, key accent moments |
| Opinion | #F2D024 | Highlights, secondary accents, hover states, sticker backgrounds |
| Air | #FFFFFF | Cards, product tiles, form fields — lifted off Paper |

### Usage Rules

**Paper (#F5F0EB)** is the default surface. It is warm, not clinical. It is not pure white — never swap it for #FFFFFF as a background. The warmth is what separates TPL from generic ecommerce.

**Ink (#1A1A1A)** is the default type color. All headings, all body, all prices. Do not use grey for type — this brand does not whisper.

**Loud (#E63B2E)** is for moments that need to act. Primary CTA buttons. Sale/drop badges. The "add to cart" button. Price display on sale items. Use it sparingly enough that it always signals action. Do not use it as a background for entire sections — it becomes noise.

**Opinion (#F2D024)** is for emphasis and energy. Highlight bars behind key copy. Hover states. Secondary badges. Sticker label backgrounds. Can be used as a full section background for promotional moments (sale announcements, new drop banners) — it's loud but it reads fun, not aggressive.

**Air (#FFFFFF)** lifts product cards off the Paper background. Product tiles sit on white so the colourful stickers read cleanly. The slight contrast between #FFFFFF cards and #F5F0EB background creates depth without shadows.

### What You Do Not Do

- Do not use gradients. Ever. This brand is flat and direct.
- Do not use the full Loud red as a page background for anything except short announcement bars.
- Do not use pastels or add tints/shades to the palette. Five colours. That's the system.
- Do not use the dark system (#1A1A1A base) for any digital surface. Dark is reserved for occasional print poster moments only (see Print section).

## Typography

### Typefaces
- **Barlow Condensed Bold / Black** — all headlines, all opinion statements, all product names, all CTAs
- **Inter Regular / Medium** — all body copy, descriptions, microcopy, navigation

### Type Hierarchy

| Role | Font | Weight | Size (mobile) | Colour |
|------|------|--------|----------------|--------|
| Opinion Headline | Barlow Condensed | Black (900) | 48-64px | Ink or Loud |
| Section Header | Barlow Condensed | Bold (700) | 32-40px | Ink |
| Product Name | Barlow Condensed | Bold (700) | 20-24px | Ink |
| Price | Barlow Condensed | Bold (700) | 20px | Loud |
| Body Copy | Inter | Regular (400) | 15-16px | Ink |
| Microcopy | Inter | Regular (400) | 13px | Ink at 60% opacity |
| CTA Button | Barlow Condensed | Bold (700) | 16px | Air on Loud bg |

### Type Rules

All caps for opinion headlines and CTAs. Title case for product names. Sentence case for body copy and descriptions. Never decorative fonts. Never script. Never thin weights — this brand does not whisper.

The headline treatment for "SMALL OBJECTS. BIG OPINIONS." and "WEAR YOUR OPINION." should always be full-width, all caps, Barlow Condensed Black. These are the brand's loudest statements — give them the space.

## Photography Direction

### The Problem with White Background Ecommerce
Every sticker brand on Meesho, Flipkart, Amazon uses white backgrounds with a centered product. TPL cannot look like that. The paper-warm background differentiates us visually from marketplace listings while remaining light and accessible.

### The TPL Product Shot
**Surface:** Warm cream posterboard (#F5F0EB or closest available physical equivalent). Not pure white. The warmth is intentional.

**Lighting:** Single desk lamp, positioned 45 degrees to the side and slightly above. Not directly overhead. The slight shadow this creates gives depth without being dramatic. Phone camera, portrait mode off, no beauty filter.

**Composition:** Do not center a single sticker on a blank background — that's a marketplace listing, not a brand photo. Arrange stickers in groups. Scatter them slightly. Let them overlap at the edges. The abundance IS the product story.

**Props allowed:** Yellow washi tape strips. A worn pencil. A coffee cup edge in the frame. A notebook corner. Things that belong to the person who buys TPL. Nothing styled or aspirational — things that are actually on someone's desk.

**The Opinion Shot format:** One sticker, close-cropped, filling 80% of the frame, on cream. The text of the sticker IS the composition. This format works for product detail shots and social.

**What you never do:**
- No dark backgrounds
- No flat lay on marble (reads premium gift brand)
- No hands with perfect manicures (reads styled/fake)
- No moody shadows (reads editorial/premium)
- No products floating on white with drop shadows (reads marketplace)

### Lifestyle Photography
Someone's actual desk, bag, laptop, water bottle with TPL stickers on them. Candid-feeling. Shot like a friend took it, not a photographer. The stickers should look like they've been there a while — not freshly applied and perfectly placed.

## Display & Packaging Format

### The Counter Display Box
The primary retail/market format. A tilted cardboard display box (Korean sticker box format) that holds the individual sticker packs, sits on a counter or market stall table, and IS the display.

**Box exterior — colors:**
- Primary colorway: Opinion yellow (#F2D024) box body, Ink (#1A1A1A) typography
- Secondary colorway: Loud red (#E63B2E) box body, Air (#FFFFFF) typography
- Tertiary colorway: Paper (#F5F0EB) box body, Ink typography with Loud accent spine

**Box exterior — typography:**
- Header panel: "THE PRODUCT LAB" in Barlow Condensed Black, full caps
- Subline: "WEAR YOUR OPINION." in Barlow Condensed Bold
- Side panels: opinion statements in large condensed type

**Individual sticker packs:**
Kraft paper envelope with a printed label. Cream label, ink type, Loud or Opinion accent for the pack name. Pack names in Barlow Condensed Bold all caps.

### The Matchbook Format
Retained for gifting SKUs. Three colorways: cream/red, yellow/black, red/cream. Used as a gift format — the matchbook IS the presentation.

## The Stall / Pop-Up Environment

**The build:** Red (#E63B2E) plastic milk crates stacked as shelving. Overhead or backdrop: cream fabric or paper. Products create the visual noise — no expensive fixtures needed. Total material cost: ₹2,000-3,000.

**Signage:** A4 or A3 prints on cream paper. Bold condensed type. Black on cream, or Opinion yellow blocks. Pin or tape directly to backdrop. No frames.

**The selfie moment:** A mirror with TPL stickers on the frame. Bold type across the top: "WEAR YOUR OPINION." Yellow at the bottom: "theproductlab.in". This is the in-store UGC engine.

**Color rule for the stall:** One dominant color (red crates) + cream surface + the products do the rest.

## Print

The one context where dark reappears — as a deliberate contrast moment, not a default.

**Posters:** Three formats — Opinion Yellow bg with Ink type, Loud Red bg with Air type, and occasionally Ink bg with Air type for high-contrast announcement moments.

**The opinion poster formula:** Full-bleed background color. One statement in Barlow Condensed Black, maximum two lines, filling the poster edge to edge. Nothing else.

## What Changed vs. Darkroom

**What was lost:**
- The editorial premium feel
- Strong contrast between dark background and colourful products
- Differentiation from light/bright competitors on the dark axis

**What was gained:**
- Alignment between how the brand looks and what it costs
- Photography is achievable for a solo operator with a phone
- The warm cream base feels like paper — where stickers live
- Broader appeal without sacrificing boldness

**The core trade:** Prestige for accessibility. Right trade for this brand.

---

## Anti-Drift Rules

Things that accidentally make this system look premium:

1. **Too much white space** — abundance is the message. Dense product displays, not minimalist layouts.
2. **Thin typography** — never use Barlow Condensed Regular or Inter Light for anything prominent.
3. **Muted photography** — if the product photos look desaturated or moody, reshoot.
4. **Small CTAs** — buttons should be chunky and loud. Loud red, full width on mobile.
5. **Careful layouts** — TPL is not precious. Stickers can overlap. Type can be big.
6. **Too few colours** — using only cream and ink makes it minimalist. Red and yellow must appear above the fold on every page.

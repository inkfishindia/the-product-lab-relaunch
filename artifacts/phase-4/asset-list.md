## Part 1: Photography Rig Setup

Set this up once. Shoot everything in one or two sessions. The consistency of a single setup is more valuable than varied setups.

### What to Buy

| Item | Spec | Where | Cost |
|------|------|-------|------|
| Cream posterboard | A2 or A1 size, matte finish, not glossy. Closest physical colour to #F5F0EB. If you find warm ivory, buy that. Do not buy bright white — it reads clinical. | Stationery shop, Art supplies store (Bengaluru: Camel Art, Art Lounge, or any stationery on MG Road/SP Road area) | ₹30–60 per sheet. Buy 5 sheets. |
| Desk lamp | Adjustable arm, bulb must be replaceable. Ikea TERTIAL or any local equivalent. Needs an E27 socket. | Ikea (Bengaluru), or local hardware store | ₹500–800 |
| Warm white LED bulb | 2700K–3000K. NOT daylight (5000K+). 8–10W. GLS shape (round bulb). The colour temperature is what makes the cream surface feel warm, not clinical. | Any electrical shop | ₹80–120 |
| Yellow washi tape | 2–3 rolls in yellow and neutral tones. Used as props in some shots — tape strip across a corner of a sticker group, or across a notebook edge. | Stationery shop | ₹80–150 |
| Worn pencil | One pencil, slightly used. Not a new sharpened pencil — it will look staged. Genuinely used. | Already on your desk |  — |

**Total one-time cost: approximately ₹700–1,200**

### Lamp Position

Place the posterboard flat on a table. Position the lamp so the bulb is to the upper-left of your shooting position, at roughly 45 degrees sideways and 45 degrees elevated. The lamp should not be directly above (kills depth) and not directly to the side (too dramatic). The target shadow is soft, falls to the right, gives dimension without drama.

Test shot: place one sticker on the posterboard. Take a photo. The sticker should be the brightest element. There should be a visible but soft shadow. If the shadow is hard-edged, the lamp is too close — move it back 20cm. If there is no shadow at all, the lamp is too far left or too overhead.

### Phone Settings

These apply to every product shot in this brief.

- Camera app: use the native app, not Instagram camera, not Snapchat.
- Portrait mode: OFF. Portrait mode adds lens blur to the background, which looks fake when the background is a flat surface. The blur also softens sticker text.
- Beauty filter / face retouching: OFF. Check settings once and leave off.
- HDR: OFF. HDR blends multiple exposures. It can make flat subjects look washed out.
- Night mode: OFF. You are in a controlled light environment. Night mode creates blur on static subjects.
- Grid lines: ON. Helps with composition alignment.
- Shoot at maximum resolution. Downscale in Canva, never upscale.
- White balance: tap the posterboard to set exposure and focus on the surface before composing your sticker arrangement, then recompose. Or set white balance manually to 3000K if your phone allows it.
- Aspect ratio: shoot in 4:3 (the native phone ratio). Crop to specific formats in Canva later.

### Post-Processing

Canva (free tier) is the only tool needed.

- Import photo. Adjust brightness: +5 to +10 if needed. The cream surface should look warm, not dull.
- Adjust contrast: +5 to +10. Sticker text should be crisp and readable.
- No filters. No vignettes. No grain effects.
- Crop to required format (specs in each section below).
- Export as JPEG. Quality setting: high (not maximum — file size matters for site LCP).

## Part 2: Product Photography Shot List

These are the images the site needs to function. Organised by page. Every image is tied to a specific site location from Kurt's site build brief.

### 2.1 The Opinion Shot — Format Spec

This is the single most important photography format in this brief. Sean defined it. It is used for product tiles, PDP hero images, and social posts.

**Setup:**
- One sticker placed on the cream posterboard.
- Sticker fills 80% of the frame. Get close. The text of the sticker IS the composition.
- Position the sticker slightly left of center or slightly right of center — not perfectly centered. Perfect centering looks like a marketplace listing.
- Angle the sticker 2–5 degrees off-horizontal. Very slight rotation. Not dramatically tilted — barely off.
- Light from upper-left as described in Part 1.
- No props in this shot. Just sticker on cream.

**Shoot dimensions:** As close to 1:1 square as possible. Crop to 1080x1080px in Canva.

**Required for all 20 sticker texts.** See the full list in Part 3.

### 2.2 Homepage Hero Image (P0)

**What it needs to show:** Multiple stickers scattered on the cream posterboard. 5–7 different sticker designs visible. They should slightly overlap at the edges. They should look like someone placed them naturally, not styled them perfectly. Abundance is the message — the variety and density of opinions is the product story.

**Setup:**
- Lay out 6–8 stickers on the posterboard. Scatter them. Let corners overlap.
- Include at least one sticker from each category: something petty (YOUR LOSS, NOTED. IGNORED.), something unbothered (MAIN CHARACTER, SOFT LIFE ONLY), something self-aware (DOING MY BEST, SENT FROM MY ANXIETY).
- Shoot from directly above (bird's eye). Phone perpendicular to the surface. Use the grid lines to keep it level.
- Lamp at 45 degrees as normal — it will create soft shadows under the sticker edges, which adds depth.
- No other props in this specific shot.

**Crop and export:**
- Desktop web hero: 1440x600px JPEG. Crop from the centre of the scattered arrangement.
- Mobile web hero: 750x900px JPEG. Taller crop of the same arrangement.
- Both are needed. Shoot in 4:3 and you can crop both from one image.

**Volume:** 2 variations. Shoot with slightly different sticker arrangements so there is a backup.

**Priority: P0.** The homepage does not go live without this.

## Part 3: The Opinion Shot — Full Series (All 20 Stickers)

This format is the workhorse of the content system. One sticker, close-cropped, filling 80% of frame, on cream. Shoot all 20 in a single session.

**Session plan:** Set up the rig once. Shoot all 20 back to back. For each sticker:
1. Place sticker on cream posterboard. Angle 2–5 degrees off-horizontal.
2. Fill 80% of the frame with the sticker. The text is the whole point.
3. Take 3 shots with slightly different framing (closer, centred, slightly off-centre). Keep the best.
4. Move to the next sticker without changing the lamp position.

**Total shooting time estimate:** 45–60 minutes for all 20 if the setup is already done.

**What you end up with:**
- 20 Opinion Shot images at 1080x1080px
- These serve as: grid tile images, PDP hero images (can be the same file), Instagram posts, and Story assets
- One session, 3+ channels. This is the highest-leverage shoot in this brief.

**File naming:**
`tpl-opinion-[sticker-text-slug]-01.jpg`

Examples:
- `tpl-opinion-your-loss-01.jpg`
- `tpl-opinion-main-character-01.jpg`
- `tpl-opinion-noted-ignored-01.jpg`
- `tpl-opinion-very-normal-amount-of-feelings-01.jpg`

Keep all three variants per sticker. Name alternates `-02.jpg`, `-03.jpg`. The `-01.jpg` is the approved hero file.

## Part 4: Brand and Lifestyle Photography

These images are for the homepage lifestyle section, Instagram grid, and email headers. They show the stickers in use — not floating on a surface, but attached to objects that belong to the person who buys TPL.

These shots require more planning than the Opinion Shots. Read all three setups before scheduling the session.

### 4.1 Laptop Shot (P0)

**What it shows:** A laptop screen visible in background, lid open. Two to three TPL stickers applied to the laptop lid. Shot from above or from a slight angle so both the sticker text and the laptop surface are readable.

**Setup:**
- Use your own laptop. Do not use a brand-new laptop with no scratches — it looks fake.
- Apply 3 stickers to the lid: recommend YOUR LOSS, MAIN CHARACTER, and one from the Relational set.
- Place the laptop on the cream posterboard or on a wooden desk surface (wooden desk is fine here — it adds warmth and reality).
- Place a coffee cup edge in the bottom corner of the frame. Out of focus is fine.
- A worn pencil or pen near the laptop.
- Shot from above (overhead) or from 45 degrees — test both and keep the better one.
- Natural window light preferred for this shot if available — it reads more real than desk lamp. If window light is not available, desk lamp at upper-left as normal.

**Crop:** 1080x1080px square for Instagram. 1200x628px for homepage lifestyle section.

**Priority: P0.** This is the primary lifestyle image used in social and the homepage.

### 4.2 Water Bottle Shot (P1)

**What it shows:** A plain water bottle (stainless steel or matte plastic — not a branded bottle) with one or two TPL stickers applied. The bottle on a desk or table surface. Shot straight-on or at a slight angle.

## Part 8: What Does NOT Need to Be Produced for Launch

This is explicitly what Dan should not spend time on before the store goes live.

**Do not produce:**
- Video content of any kind. No Reels, no Shorts, no product demos. Video is a post-launch content expansion, not a launch requirement. The store needs still photography.
- Behind-the-scenes content. The story of how the packaging was made, etc. This is post-launch content for the Collect/loyalty phase.
- Illustrated character content. Drop 1 is text-only stickers. Illustrated stickers are Drop 2 territory (D-011 confirms accessories only at launch, and the product hierarchy confirms text stickers first).
- Model/lifestyle photography with people. The brand does not require this at launch — it actually works against authenticity at this stage. Stickers-on-objects is the right lifestyle register.
- Brand lookbook or editorial spread. Too much time, too little return at launch scale.
- White-background product photography. The warm cream surface is the differentiator. Do not produce clinical white-background images for any channel.
- Pop-up / stall photography. The stall environment (red crates, etc.) is a future physical event format. Do not set this up or photograph it before the store is live.
- UGC templates. Templates for customer-generated content can wait until there are customers generating content.
- YouTube content. Not a launch channel. If Dan has capacity, this is Phase 6 territory.
- Packaging dielines / mockup photography. The physical packaging photography from Part 5 serves all digital needs. Detailed dieline photography (box construction, flat pack) is not a consumer-facing need.

**The test for whether something needs to be produced before launch:** Does the absence of this asset block a page from going live or a post from being published? If no, it waits.

## Part 10: Production Schedule

Dan is one person. This schedule protects him from trying to do everything at once.

**Session 1 — Canva templates (no physical setup needed)**
Build Templates 1–6. Takes approximately 2–3 hours. Can be done any time with no physical product. Do this first so the templates are ready before the shoot.

**Session 2 — Opinion Shot series (all 20 stickers)**
Physical product must exist. Set up the rig once. Shoot all 20 Opinion Shots. Takes approximately 60–90 minutes including setup. This single session delivers the content for 20 product grid tiles, 20 PDP hero images, 4 pre-launch posts, and social content pipeline.

**Session 3 — Set and packaging shots**
Shoot the 4 set flat-lays (The Loud Ones, Main Character Set, No Notes, and one gift pack packaging shot). Takes approximately 45 minutes.

**Session 4 — Lifestyle shots**
Laptop shot, water bottle shot, desk scene. These require slightly more setup time. Budget 60 minutes.

**Post-processing batch**
Import all photos to Canva. Crop and export to the formats specified. File and name according to the convention in Part 9. Send web images to Tobi in the Google Drive folder.

**Total estimated time:**
- Canva templates: 2–3 hours
- Shooting sessions: 3–4 hours across 2–3 sessions
- Post-processing: 2–3 hours
- **Total: 7–10 hours across several days. Not a single day of work.**

Spread across the build phase. The Opinion Shots and Canva templates are the absolute priority. Everything else supports and expands.

## Review Checklist Before Handing to Tobi

Before passing any web image to Tobi for upload, confirm:

- [ ] Image is warm cream background, not pure white
- [ ] No dark backgrounds in any product shot
- [ ] Sticker text is fully legible at 200px wide (the thumbnail test)
- [ ] No portrait mode blur on any product image
- [ ] File is JPEG, not PNG or HEIC
- [ ] File is named according to the convention in Part 9
- [ ] File is sized correctly for its intended use (check the spec in each section)
- [ ] No Instagram filter or colour treatment applied
- [ ] No watermarks

If any image fails this checklist, reshoot before passing it on. A low-quality image on the site at launch cannot be unseen by the first 500 visitors.

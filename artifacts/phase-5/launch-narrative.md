## Part 1: The Launch Narrative

### The 3-Line Pitch (What Dan Says When Someone Asks "What's New?")

**"The Product Lab is an accessories brand where every object has an opinion. We're making things that let you be specific about who you are without having to explain it. Card stickers, attitude keychains, and original artist designs — all made to say what you actually think."**

This is not a press release. This is Dan's north star. Use this framing in:
- WhatsApp broadcasts
- Instagram Stories
- DMs with early customers
- Email subject lines
- The about page
- Anything public-facing in the first 30 days

The narrative reframes the relaunch from "new website" to "brand with a point of view arriving." The emphasis is on attitude and specificity, not on product breadth or price.

### Why This Narrative Works

1. **It is specific.** "Accessories with opinions" cannot be confused with Dot Badges, Chumbak, or any other brand. It is ownable and defensible.

2. **It leads with the unique product.** Card stickers get mentioned first. This is the anchor — the thing no one else has.

3. **It solves the customer's job.** "Help me be specific about who I am" is more powerful than "give me a gift under ₹500."

4. **It fits in a WhatsApp message.** It can be forwarded, screenshot-ed, shared. It is native to the discovery channel.

5. **It does not require explaining the "what."** A customer does not need to know what a card sticker is before deciding they want one. The narrative does the first conversion step.

## Part 2: Pre-Launch Phase (T-30 to T-0)

The 30 days before launch are the most important 30 days. Everything that happens before the site goes live determines whether the launch succeeds or fails. The goal is to validate that card sticker pricing is defensible, the voice works at scale, and there is a real audience waiting.

### Week 1 (T-30 to T-23): Seeding & List Building

**Priority:** Build the gifting and subscriber list. Get the first 20+ product gifts into real hands.

**Actions:**

1. **Day 1 (T-30):** Finalize the 20-25 people to gift to. Use Rachel's seeding plan. These are not influencers — they are taste-setters. Active on Instagram, aesthetically coherent, 19-28, Tier 1 city. Design Yatra contacts are the first wave.

2. **Days 2-3 (T-29 to T-28):** Pack and ship the first batch of 10 gifts. Each includes:
   - 1-2 hero products (card sticker + attitude keychain or pin)
   - 1 support product (thematic keychain or magnet)
   - Handwritten note (see seeding-plan.md for copy)
   - Business card with @the.product.lab Instagram handle

3. **Days 4-7 (T-26 to T-23):** Observe what happens. Do they post? What do they share? Screenshot everything that happens and save to a "Pre-Launch Social Proof" folder.

4. **Subscriber list target by end of Week 1:** 50+ email subscribers, 30+ WhatsApp opt-ins from existing network.

   How to get them:
   - DM all previous customers. "Something new is coming. Should I add you to the first-access list?"
   - Design Yatra contacts: send a personal email to 30-40 people, invite to subscribe
   - Instagram Story CTA: "New collection drops [date]. First 100 subscribers get early access + ₹50 off."
   - WhatsApp Business set up with broadcast list ready

**Narrative messaging for this week:** Nothing public yet. Everything is private DMs and email. Keep the scarcity high — "you're on the early list" is the message.

**Metrics to watch:** Subscribe conversion rate (who says yes when asked), any organic social proof (gifted products being posted).

### Week 2 (T-23 to T-16): First Content, Second Wave Gifting

**Priority:** Post the first 3-4 pieces of pre-launch content. Seed the second wave of 10 gifts.

**Actions:**

1. **Days 1-3 (T-23 to T-21):** Post teaser series on Instagram. See content-calendar.md for exact copy, but the theme is "Something changed." Do not reveal the product yet — just the brand statement.
   - Post 1 (Reel, Tuesday 9 AM): "Small objects. Big opinions." — 30-second visual system intro
   - Post 2 (Carousel, Thursday 9 AM): "We're not doing quirky." — positioning statement slide-by-slide
   - Post 3 (Story, Saturday 4 PM): Tease the card sticker (do not show the full product)

2. **Days 4-7 (T-19 to T-16):** Ship second wave of 10 gifts. Include a slightly different product mix — test whether certain products generate more organic shares. For instance, give some people attitude keychains, others give original pins, track which gets more traction.

3. **Subscriber list target by end of Week 2:** 150+ email subscribers, 80+ WhatsApp opt-ins.

   New tactics:
   - Teasers in Instagram Stories: "Tell me your opinion [link to subscribe]"
   - Email the week-1 subscribers: "Want early access to the new drop? Forward this to a friend and you both get in early"
   - Design Yatra second-wave personal emails with the launch date announced

**Narrative shift:** Move from "something is happening" to "this is what it is." Reveal the brand positioning but not the full catalog.

**Metrics to watch:** Instagram Story click-through rate on subscribe links, organic shares from week-1 gifted products, email open rates.

### Week 3 (T-16 to T-9): Hero Products Revealed, Content Doubles

**Priority:** Reveal the card sticker and attitude collection. Test messaging and establish the voice at scale. Get to 300+ subscribers.

**Actions:**

1. **Days 1-2 (T-16 to T-15):** Post the hero product reveal.
   - Post 4 (Reel, Tuesday 9 AM): Card sticker hero Reel. Show it in wallet, being tapped at a reader, the reveal moment. Use the card sticker headline: "Your bank card has something to say."
   - Post 5 (Carousel, Thursday 9 AM): "No Filter" attitude collection reveal. Show 4-5 attitude products. Each slide is one product with the headline.

2. **Days 3-4 (T-14 to T-13):** Ship third wave of 10 gifts — this time, focus on card stickers and attitude keychains. These are the products you want generating organic proof.

3. **Days 5-7 (T-11 to T-9):** Post voice content (no product).
   - Post 6 (Static, Wednesday 11 AM): "What your keychain says about you." Typographic post with attitude product names.
   - Post 7 (Story, Friday 3 PM): "This is not for everyone. That is the point." Positioning statement with link to subscribe.

4. **Subscriber list target:** 400+ subscribers by end of week.

**Narrative development:** Shift from "we're new" to "here's what we stand for." The voice is now public and searchable.

**Metrics to watch:** Which product reveals generate the highest engagement, Instagram save rate (indicator of shareability), website traffic from Instagram clicks, subscriber growth velocity.

### Week 4 (T-9 to T-2): Behind-the-Scenes, Countdown, Final Gifting, Launch Confirmation

**Priority:** Build urgency for launch. Reach 500 subscribers. Finalize all product photography and copy on the live site.

**Actions:**

1. **Days 1-3 (T-9 to T-7):** Behind-the-scenes content and artist story.
   - Post 8 (Carousel, Monday 9 AM): "Made by artists who mean it." Show the creative process, the studio, artist names. Use this to humanize the brand.
   - Post 9 (Video/Reel, Wednesday 11 AM): Artist feature or studio tour (30-60 seconds). Make it personal, not polished.

2. **Days 4-5 (T-5 to T-4):** Final gifting wave of 5 key people (repeat gifters or people whose earlier shares performed well). This is for last-minute social proof.

3. **Days 6-7 (T-2 to T-1):** Launch announcement + countdown.
   - Day 6: Email to 500+ subscribers: "The wait is over. We launch tomorrow 9 AM." Subject line: "Tomorrow, 9 AM." (See email-whatsapp-flows.md)
   - Day 6: Instagram Story countdown — "48 hours, 24 hours, 12 hours, 6 hours"
   - Day 7: WhatsApp broadcast: "Tomorrow morning. 9 AM. We've made something good."

4. **Subscriber target:** 500+ confirmed subscribers on email + WhatsApp lists before launch.

5. **Content published before launch:** All 10 posts live. None scheduled for launch day — Day 0 gets its own fresh post.

**Narrative finalization:** The launch is no longer a surprise — it is an event people have been waiting for.

**Metrics to watch:** Email send/open rates on launch announcement, WhatsApp opt-in rate, final subscriber growth rate, Instagram engagement on countdown content.

## Part 3: Launch Week (T-0 to T+7)

Launch week is a daily ops game. Dan is checking metrics multiple times a day. Everything below is time-locked and sequenced.

### T-0 (Launch Day) — Hour by Hour

**Objective:** Get the launch in front of the audience that has been primed for 30 days. Capture the first orders. Generate social proof for the orders that come.

#### 8:45 AM (T-15 minutes)
- Final site check: all products live on production, checkout works, GA4 DebugView is on
- Confirm first 10 posts are scheduled or ready to go live in 15 minutes

#### 9:00 AM (T-0)
- **Publish launch Reel.** Title: "We're live." Format: 30 seconds, showing 3-4 hero products in quick cuts. Music: something confident but not trendy. No talking head. Post with caption: "It's here. Small objects. Big opinions. Shop now." Include the link in the first comment.
- **Send email to 500+ subscribers:** Subject: "It's live." See exact copy in email-whatsapp-flows.md. Send immediately after Instagram Reel posts.
- **Screenshot the Reel's first engagement metrics at 9:30 AM:** Views, likes, comments. Save to monitoring folder.

#### 9:05 AM (T+5 minutes)
- **WhatsApp broadcast to all subscribers:** "The Product Lab is live. Card stickers. Attitude keychains. Original artist pins. First 50 orders get 10% off. [link]" Use the exact template from email-whatsapp-flows.md.

#### 10:00 AM (T+1 hour)
- Check Shopify dashboard or Fynd analytics:
  - Sessions so far (target: 50+)
  - Add-to-cart events (target: 10+)
  - Completed orders (target: 2+)
- Check Instagram: any replies, DMs, tags from the seeded customers?

#### 12:00 PM (T+3 hours)
- **Publish Instagram Story:** "X people bought in the first 2 hours." (If X > 0, this works. If X = 0, post something different — see contingency below). Include a link sticker to shop.
- Check Instagram DMs. Respond to all questions within 30 minutes. Response template: short, friendly, in brand voice. Example: "Hey! The [product name] usually ships in 3-5 days, and you can track it on the shipping email. It looks good IRL too — promise."

#### 6:00 PM (T+9 hours)
- **Daily metrics review.** Check and record:
  - Total sessions (target: 200+)
  - Total add-to-cart events (target: 30+)
  - Total orders (target: 5+)
  - Top product (which product is in the most carts?)
  - Traffic source (Instagram, direct, WhatsApp, email)
  - Conversion rate (orders / sessions)
  - AOV (average order value — target: ₹350+)

#### 8:00 PM (T+11 hours)
- **Instagram Story post:** "X orders in the first day." (Real number, real names if they want to be tagged). Add screenshot of order confirmation if customer okays it. This is social proof for Day 2 browsers.
- **Respond to all DMs and comments that came in between 12 PM and 8 PM.** Priority on product questions and complaints. Aim for <1 hour response time.

#### 10:00 PM (T+13 hours)
- Final check: no critical errors on site, no surge in support requests, no payment failures
- Prepare Day 1 metrics report

### T+1 to T+6 (Days 1-6 Post-Launch)

**Daily operating rhythm:**

**9:00 AM:** Check overnight metrics. Post new content per the content-calendar.md daily post schedule.

**12:00 PM:** Instagram Stories update — share customer feedback, testimonials, or user-generated content if any exists.

**6:00 PM:** Record daily metrics (sessions, orders, AOV, conversion rate, top product). Add to a simple spreadsheet or Google Sheet for trend tracking.

**8:00 PM:** Respond to all DMs and comments from the day. Answer product questions. Do not ignore complaints — respond within 2 hours, even to say "we're looking into this."

**10:00 PM:** Check for any critical issues. If order volume is very high, monitor for fulfillment delays or payment failures.

**Backup WhatsApp sends** (optional, based on engagement):
- Day 2: "Thanks for the orders yesterday. Next drop: [date]"
- Day 4: "Only [X] left of the [product name]. Going fast."
- Day 6: "Last chance before we restock." (If low stock is real)

**Contingency: If sales are slow on Day 0 or Day 1:**
- Do not publish panic posts
- DM the seeded customers directly: "Hey, we just launched. Would love to get your take on the new products. Honestly."
- Post a user-generated content piece if any of the pre-launch gifted products generated photos (ask permission first)
- Send a second email on Day 2: "Some favorites from today:" + best-selling product + testimonial if exists

### T+7 (End of Week 1)

**Metrics review:** Have Dan sit down for 30 minutes and review all Week 1 data.

**Questions to answer:**
- How many customers? (target: 15-30)
- What is the AOV? (target: ₹350+)
- Which products are winning? (card stickers or attitude keychains?)
- What is the card sticker pricing feeling like? (Is ₹149-199 right, or should it go up to ₹249?)
- Which traffic source is converting best? (Instagram, email, WhatsApp, organic)
- Are there any support issues? (shipping, payment, returns)
- How many of the 500 subscribers made a purchase? (target: >5%, so 25+)

**Actions based on Week 1 data:**
- If card sticker sales are strong and conversion is >2%, consider a price test to ₹249 for Week 2
- If attitude keychains are outselling others, feature them more prominently in Week 2 Instagram content
- If a specific traffic source is winning (e.g., WhatsApp referrals), double down on that channel in Week 2
- If conversion is <1%, something is broken — review site analytics for drop-off points

**Week 2 planning:** Create a brief written plan for Days 8-14 based on Week 1 learnings.

## Part 4: Post-Launch Weeks (T+8 to T+30)

The first month is about learning and optimization, not growth at scale. Dan's job is to notice what works and double it.

### Weeks 2-4: Core Activities

**3x per week (Mon/Wed/Fri, 9 AM):** New Instagram content (per content-calendar.md)

**2x per week (Tuesday, Friday):** WhatsApp broadcast (product update, testimonial, or reminder)

**1x per week (Friday 6 PM):** Metrics review and next-week planning

**Daily:** Respond to DMs within 2 hours. Answer all product questions.

**Weekly:** Check Clarity and GA4 for user behavior insights. Are people bottlenecking at checkout? Are they clicking product photos?

### Drop 2 Announcement (T+21 to T+28)

**When:** After the first 500 customers have moved through (or after Day 21, whichever comes first).

**What:** A new product drop — could be a new color of card stickers, a new artist series of pins, or a "Sold Out" restock announcement for the best-sellers.

**How:**
- Tease on Instagram Stories: "Something new, Friday" (posted Wednesday evening)
- Email: "Exclusive access: new drop launching Friday" (sent Thursday morning to customers only)
- WhatsApp: "New artist series drops Friday 9 AM. Only 100 made." (sent Thursday evening)
- Launch with a Reel on Friday at 9 AM, same as Day 0

**Why:** Establish the drop cadence early. Make it feel like a brand that does regular launches, not a site that updates randomly.

## Part 5: Metrics Dan Checks Every Day (T+0 to T+30)

These are the five numbers that matter in the first 30 days. Dan creates a simple Google Sheet with these columns and fills them in at 6 PM every day:

| Date | Sessions | Add-to-Cart | Orders | AOV (₹) | Conversion % |
|------|----------|-------------|--------|---------|--------------|
| 2026-04-05 | 120 | 15 | 3 | ₹385 | 2.5% |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

**What Dan looks for:**

1. **Sessions:** Are they growing? Week 1: 200-300 sessions/day. Week 2: 300-500. Week 3: 400-700. By Week 4, plateau is normal.

2. **Conversion rate:** Is it improving? Week 1: 1-2% is good. Week 2: aim for 1.5-2.5%. By Week 4, shooting for 2%+ and stable.

3. **AOV:** Is it growing? Week 1: ₹300-350. Week 2: ₹350-400. Week 3-4: ₹400+ (bundles working).

4. **Top product:** Which product appears in the most orders? This tells Dan what to feature in Week 2-3 content.

5. **Traffic source:** Which channel is sending the most converting traffic? (Breakdown: Instagram, Email, WhatsApp, Organic, Direct). Double down on the winner.

**Weekly goal review:**
- End of Week 1: 15-30 orders, ₹350+ AOV
- End of Week 2: 40-70 total orders, card sticker pricing validated, Word-of-mouth referrals appearing (customers tagging friends)
- End of Week 3: 80-130 total orders, 1-2 repeat customers, clear top-product winner
- End of Week 4: 120-200 total orders, 3-5 repeat customers, conversion stable at 2%+, ready to announce Drop 2

## Part 6: What Success Looks Like

If the launch works, by Day 30 Dan will have:

- 120-200 customers
- Average order value of ₹400+
- 2%+ conversion rate
- Card sticker pricing validated at ₹149-199 (or higher if demand is very strong)
- A clear winning product (likely card stickers or attitude keychains)
- A clear winning traffic source (likely Instagram + WhatsApp)
- 5-10 repeat customers (proof of retention)
- Organic word-of-mouth happening (friends of customers visiting the site)
- 10+ pieces of user-generated content or testimonials
- A clear drop-2 announcement ready to go
- Confidence that the brand positioning works

This is launch success. Growth comes later.

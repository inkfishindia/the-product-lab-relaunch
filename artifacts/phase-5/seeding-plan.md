## Part 1: Seeding Philosophy

This is not influencer marketing. This is genuine gifting to real people.

The goal is simple: get 20-30 real products into the hands of real people who will use them, love them, and organically tell their friends. No contracts. No "post within 48 hours." No affiliate links or discount codes attached to their names. Just: here's something we made, we think you'll get it, we'd love your honest feedback.

**Why this works:**
- Influencer marketing fails at scale because audiences know it is paid. They scroll past.
- Genuine gifting works because if someone you actually like and trust tells you "I got this and I love it," you listen.
- The best social proof is your friend posting a photo of something they bought, unprompted, with zero promotion.

**The theory:** If the product is genuinely good, and the person who receives it is genuinely someone who would appreciate it, they will post. They will tag you. They will tell their friends. They will do this because they want to, not because you paid them.

**The constraint:** This only works if you gift to the right people. Taste-setters, not follower-counts. Identity-driven people, not marketing-savvy people. People who actually use the objects they buy, not people who buy things to photograph.

## Part 2: Target Profile for Seeded Products

Dan is not looking for a list of names. He is looking for a TYPE of person to identify and gift to.

### The Taste-Setter (Who Gets Gifted)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 19-28 (core target segment)
- Location: Tier 1 or Tier 2 city (Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi, Hyderabad, Pune, Chandigarh, etc.)
- Instagram following: 500-10,000 (not a micro-influencer, not a celebrity, just an actual person)
- Posting frequency: Active, but not obsessive (3-5 posts per week, organic engagement in comments)

**Behavioral signals:**
- Posts about objects they own: laptop stickers, pins on a board, things on their desk, what they carry
- Aesthetic coherence: their feed has a visible point of view (not just "pretty pictures," but "these are the things I like")
- Engagement authenticity: comments on friends' posts, replies in Stories, participates in DMs (not just broadcast followers)
- Not a professional creator: no "Collabs inquiries" link in bio, no sponsored posts, no "link in bio" for affiliate content

**What they share:**
- Photos of their bag and what's in it
- Laptop setups with stickers and decals
- Keychain collections or desk arrangements
- Outfit posts where accessories are visible
- Design things: typography, packaging, object photography

**What they talk about:**
- Their aesthetic or vibe (not "influencer voice," but genuine personality)
- Support for independent creators and small brands
- Their opinions on things (not afraid to say what they don't like)
- Subcultures or identity communities (K-Pop, anime, gaming, sneaker culture, design communities)

**How to identify them:**
Dan scrolls his current Instagram follower list and looks for 20-30 people who match the above. He is not looking for famous people or people with 100K followers. He is looking for people who are real, who have taste, and who will honestly react to a gift.

**Example recognition:**
- Found via Design Yatra community (active, design-literate)
- Person who liked/commented on early Product Lab posts (already signal they care about the brand)
- Friend of a friend who has been mentioned as "you would love The Product Lab"
- Anyone in the Bangalore creative/design community who is active on Instagram and does not seem like a professional influencer

## Part 3: Design Yatra Contact Activation

Design Yatra is the primary seeding network (documented in D-013). These are people who already care about design, already support independent makers, already have taste.

### How to Activate Design Yatra Contacts

**Step 1: Identify the right people in the community**

Not everyone in Design Yatra gets a gift. The criteria:
- Active participant in the community (has attended events, commented on posts, engaged publicly)
- Instagram presence (has a public profile, posts regularly, has 500+ followers)
- Early signal of interest in The Product Lab (followed, liked earlier posts, or came up in personal recommendation)

Target: 5-10 people from this community for the initial wave.

**Step 2: Personalized, non-transactional outreach**

Email or DM (not impersonal):

"Hey [name] —

I'm Dan from The Product Lab (the accessories brand launching next month). I've been following your work on Design Yatra, and I really respect your point of view on [specific thing you noticed — their aesthetic, their posts, their taste].

I've been building a new collection of accessories — card stickers, attitude keychains, original artist pins — and I think you'd genuinely appreciate it. No strings attached. No "please post this." Just: I made something, I think you might like it, and I'd love your honest feedback.

If you're interested, I'll send something your way next week. And if you're not interested, zero worries — I just wanted to reach out directly.

Either way, thanks for the work you do in this community.

— Dan"

**This email works because:**
- It is personal (specific reference to their work, not a template)
- It is clear about what you want (feedback, not a performance)
- It has zero expectation of a post
- It shows respect (acknowledges their taste first)
- It gives them an easy out (no pressure)

**Step 3: Ship thoughtfully**

Send a physical package with:
- 1-2 hero products (card sticker + attitude keychain, or pin + card sticker)
- 1 support product (thematic keychain or magnet)
- Printed business card (with @the.product.lab handle)
- Handwritten note (see copy section below)

Ship 3-4 days after the email, so it feels timely but not rushed.

**Step 4: Light follow-up (not pushy)**

One week after shipping, send one simple DM:

"Hey — did the package arrive? Curious what you think, no pressure to post anything. Just want to know if you actually like using it."

This is the only follow-up. If they post, great. If they don't, you've still made a connection with someone whose taste you respect.

## Part 4: Gifting Package Contents & Copy

### What Goes in the Box

**Products:**
- 1 hero product (card sticker ₹150-200 value or attitude keychain ₹249 value) — choose based on their known preferences
- 1 hero product variant (if giving a card sticker, include both half-card and full-card versions)
- 1 support product (thematic keychain or magnet, ₹199-249 value)
- Quantity: 1 of each, not bulk. A gift, not a wholesale package.

**Packaging:**
- Use the same packaging that will ship to customers (builds brand experience consistency)
- No plastic filler — use tissue paper or kraft paper
- Label: simple, branded, not loud
- Condition: this needs to feel like a gift, not a promotional shipment

**Insert/Note Copy:**

The handwritten note is the most important element. It is the only personal touch. It is also where the voice matters most — because this is a private moment, not a public broadcast.

**Note 1 (For taste-setters in Design Yatra):**

"Hey [name] —

I made these because accessories are the most personal things we carry. Your laptop, your phone — they could be anyone's. But your keychain? Your card? That's 100% you.

The card sticker takes five minutes to apply, and it stays there. Every time you pay, it says something about who you are. The keychain is the same idea — smaller, wearable, shareable.

I think you'll get it. And if you use it, I'd love to see what you do with it.

Made in Bengaluru. Made to last.

— Dan

P.S. These ship with artist names because the designs come from real people. The [product name] is by [artist name]. Support independent work."

This note is:
- Conversational (not corporate)
- Explains the "why" (helps them understand the philosophy)
- Gives them permission to use it (doesn't ask them to post)
- Includes the artist credit (reinforces the brand promise)
- Signed personally (not "@the.product.lab")

**Note 2 (For taste-setters outside Design Yatra):**

"Hey [name] —

I've been making a new collection of accessories — things with opinions, not just things with aesthetics.

You came to mind immediately. I think you'll get why.

Try the card sticker on your card. Clip the keychain to your bag. Tell me what you think.

— Dan"

This version is shorter because it is for people who might already know Dan or be closer to him socially.

**Business card (inside the package):**

One simple printed business card:
- Front: @the.product.lab + website
- Back: Simple tagline or QR code to shop

No fancy design needed. Plain, clean, useful.

## Part 5: The Ask (If Any)

Dan's philosophy: do not ask for anything except honesty.

### If They Post Naturally

If they post a photo or share content:
- Reply with genuine thanks (not a link, not an emoji, just a real reply)
- Screenshot the post, save it to "Pre-Launch Social Proof" folder
- That is all. No "tag your friends," no "use this code," no affiliate nonsense.

### If They Don't Post

That is also fine. They got a gift, they either use it or they don't, and either way, you have insight into whether the product resonates.

The goal is not 100% of seeded customers posting. The goal is real, authentic social proof that says: "This person got something from The Product Lab and they genuinely liked it enough to share."

## Part 6: Building the Subscriber List (500+ Before Launch)

The seeding plan is not just about products — it is about building the audience list that gets early access.

### Subscriber List Channels & Tactics

**Channel 1: Email**

Target: 400+ email subscribers before launch.

**How to get them:**

1. **Existing customers** (T-30)
   - Email anyone who bought from The Product Lab previously
   - Subject: "Something new. Want early access?"
   - Copy: "We're launching a new collection in 30 days. First-access subscribers get early preview and 10% off. In or out?"
   - Expected: 30-50 early signups from existing base

2. **Design Yatra network outreach** (T-25)
   - Personal emails to 50-100 Design Yatra members who have shown interest
   - Subject: "New accessories launch coming"
   - Copy: Mix of personal note + signup link
   - Expected: 30-50 signups from this network

3. **Instagram Story CTA** (T-20 onwards)
   - Include a clickable link sticker in Stories linking to an email signup page
   - Copy: "New collection drops [date]. First 100 subscribers get early access + 10% off."
   - Timing: Post CTA every 2-3 days in Stories
   - Expected: 100-150 signups from Instagram followers

4. **Website signup form** (T-30)
   - Simple form on the pre-launch holding page or landing page
   - Headline: "The new collection arrives [date]. Get early access."
   - Fields: Email + optional (name, city, interest area)
   - Expected: 50-100 signups from organic traffic

5. **WhatsApp Business broadcasts** (T-20 onwards)
   - DM existing customers asking if they want to be added to the launch broadcast list
   - Copy: "Send us a WhatsApp to [number] and we'll add you to the launch list."
   - Expected: 50-100 signups from this channel

**Email service:** Klaviyo (recommended) or Mailchimp. Set up a simple double-opt-in workflow. Send welcome email immediately after signup (see email-whatsapp-flows.md for copy).

---

**Channel 2: WhatsApp**

Target: 200+ WhatsApp subscribers before launch.

**How to get them:**

1. **Personal invitations to contacts**
   - Dan DMing everyone in his phone or existing customer list
   - Message: "Hey — launching something new next month. Want to be in the WhatsApp group for first access?"
   - Expected: 30-50 direct opt-ins

2. **QR code in Instagram bio or Stories**
   - Add a WhatsApp QR code in Stories with CTA: "Get launch day updates on WhatsApp"
   - Users scan the code, it opens WhatsApp with your number, they opt in
   - Expected: 50-100 opt-ins

3. **Email to existing customer base**
   - Email previous customers: "Launch day access via WhatsApp? Reply with your number or DM us."
   - Expected: 50-100 opt-ins

4. **Word-of-mouth from seeded customers**
   - As gift recipients share the brand, some will ask how to stay updated
   - Word-of-mouth: "There's a WhatsApp list for new drops"
   - Expected: 30-50 opt-ins

**WhatsApp platform:** Gupshup or MSG91 for broadcast lists. Set up a simple broadcast subscription so Dan can send updates without storing every individual chat.

1. **Accountability:** Dan knows who he has seeded and when to follow up
2. **ROI measurement:** He can see which products generate more organic shares
3. **Documentation:** He has a record of all gifting for the business

### Social Proof Folder

Create a Google Drive folder called "Pre-Launch Social Proof" and save:
- Screenshots of all organic posts from seeded customers (even if not perfect photos)
- Comments and replies from their followers
- DMs or testimonials
- The date and link to each post

This becomes content for the launch (with permission) and proof that real people love the products.

## Part 9: Budget for Seeding

Seeding is the one "paid" channel in this launch plan, but it is not paid advertising — it is paid product.

**Cost calculation:**

- 30 gifts × average product value of ₹600 per gift (card sticker + keychain + pin): ₹18,000
- Packaging & shipping per gift: ₹100 × 30: ₹3,000
- Design Yatra personal outreach (coffee meetings if any): ₹2,000
- **Total budget: ₹23,000 (~$275)**

This is a one-time cost that generates:
- 30 real people experiencing the product
- 10-15 organic social posts (estimated)
- 500+ email/WhatsApp subscribers
- Real testimonials and social proof
- Community relationships built

Compare this to a single sponsored Instagram post (₹10,000-30,000 for a 100K+ account) and seeding is the better deal. You get authenticity, real social proof, and community building instead of a one-off promotion.

## Part 10: What Success Looks Like

By T-0 (launch day), if seeding worked:

✓ 30 real people have The Product Lab products in their hands
✓ 10-15 of them have posted organically on Instagram (without being asked)
✓ 500+ email and WhatsApp subscribers waiting for launch
✓ 10+ pieces of genuine social proof (photos, testimonials, DMs)
✓ Word-of-mouth is happening ("My friend got this, I should buy it")
✓ Community feels real, not manufactured

On launch day, Dan goes live with pre-built social proof. The first-time visitor to the site sees real people using real products, which dramatically increases trust and conversion.

This is the entire seeding strategy.

## Campaign Brief

**Goal:** Build a waiting audience before the store is live. Validate the card sticker as a hero product. Reach 500+ subscribers before Drop 1.

**Operator:** Dan (sole operator). All content produced by Dan + Casey with Chase's calendar as the blueprint. No paid media in pre-launch — organic only.

**Platforms:** Instagram (primary), WhatsApp Business (broadcast), email.

**Duration:** 30 days before launch (T-30 → T-0). Launch date TBD — to be confirmed by Dan. All timings below are relative (T-minus).

**Narrative spine:** "Something changed. We rebuilt something. Not bigger. Sharper." — This moves from mystery → positioning → product reveal → anticipation → launch.

---

## The 5-Act Teaser Arc

The campaign runs in five acts. Each act has a specific emotional job and audience target. Content from Chase's calendar (`artifacts/phase-5/content-calendar.md`) maps to these acts.

---

### Act 1: Signal (T-30 to T-24) — "Something's different"

**Emotional job:** Awareness. Create a sense that something has shifted without explaining what.

**Who hears this first:** Existing followers, past customers, Dan's personal network, Design Yatra contacts via DM.

**Channels:** Instagram Stories, personal DMs, WhatsApp broadcast to Dan's close contacts.

**What goes out:**
- Instagram Story: dark product teaser. No brand name. No product name. Just a close-up of a product on a dark surface with white text: *"Something changed."*
- DM to 20-30 people (past customers, Design Yatra contacts): *"I rebuilt The Product Lab. New direction. Something you haven't seen in accessories before. Want early access before it goes public?"* — personal tone, no copy-paste feel.
- WhatsApp broadcast to existing contacts: same message, personal register.

**What NOT to do in Act 1:** Do not post to feed. Do not explain. Do not reveal products or pricing. Mystery is the only asset here.

**Metric to watch:** DM reply rate. If >30% reply "yes" to early access invite, the signal is strong.

---

### Act 2: Position (T-23 to T-17) — "This is what we're not"

**Emotional job:** Differentiation. Make the brand point of view undeniable before any product is shown.

**Who hears this:** Instagram followers + reach beyond (Reels/Carousel format helps distribution).

**Channels:** Instagram feed (Reel + Carousel), Instagram Stories (subscribe CTA).

**What goes out:**

**Post 1 — Reel (T-27, Tuesday 9 AM):** "Small objects. Big opinions." — 30-second visual brand intro. Dark product shots, Barlow Condensed text overlays. No product names. Just the aesthetic and the brand statement. Caption: see `content-calendar.md` Post 1.

**Post 2 — Carousel (T-25, Thursday 9 AM):** "We're not doing quirky." — 4-slide positioning statement. Each slide is one claim, white text on dark bg. Caption: see `content-calendar.md` Post 2.

**Post 3 — Story (T-23, Saturday 4 PM):** Card sticker partial reveal. Show the card, not the design. Story 3 has a subscribe link. This is the first subscriber CTA of the campaign.

**Subscribe page goes live:** Coming soon page live at this point. Email capture active. WhatsApp opt-in number active.

**Subscriber target by end of Act 2:** 100+ email, 50+ WhatsApp opt-ins.

---

### Act 3: Reveal (T-16 to T-9) — "Here's what it is"

**Emotional job:** Product desire. Move the audience from "interested in the brand" to "I want that specific thing."

**Hero product focus:** Card sticker first. Then attitude keychain. Everything else is supporting.

**Channels:** Instagram feed (Reel + Carousel + Static), Stories daily, WhatsApp broadcast to list.

**What goes out:**

**Post 4 — Reel (T-16, Tuesday 9 AM):** Card sticker hero Reel. 30-45 seconds. Show it going on the card. Show the tap moment at a reader. Show the reaction. No voiceover — just Barlow Condensed text overlays. This is the most important piece of content in the entire pre-launch. Every rupee of effort goes into making it good.

*Shoot brief for Dan:* Dark surface, wallet on surface, single lamp from upper right. Card with sticker pulled out of wallet in one motion. Tap at a reader (can be any reader). Reverse angle showing the sticker. Speed: 1.5x with cuts on beats.

**Post 5 — Carousel (T-14, Thursday 9 AM):** "No Filter" attitude collection reveal. 5-6 slides. Each slide = one attitude product, headline copy from PDP (see `copy-system.md` PDP templates). Caption: see `content-calendar.md` Post 5.

**Post 6 — Static/Typographic (T-12, Wednesday 11 AM):** "What your keychain says about you." Voice post — no product photo. Just Barlow Condensed attitude product names stacked on dark bg. Copy does the work.

**Post 7 — Story (T-10, Friday 3 PM):** "This is not for everyone. That is the point." Subscribe CTA. Urgency: "Launch is [X days] away. First 100 subscribers get early access + ₹50 off."

**WhatsApp broadcast (T-11):** Product reveal message to list. "The card sticker is what we've been building toward. Watch this." + link to Reel.

**Subscriber target by end of Act 3:** 350+ email, 180+ WhatsApp.

**Gifting (parallel):** Second and third waves of 20+ seed gifts ship in Act 3 (see Rachel's seeding plan). Focus on card stickers and attitude keychains.

---

### Act 4: Anticipation (T-8 to T-2) — "It's almost here"

**Emotional job:** Urgency. Create genuine FOMO. Activate the subscriber list. Convert fence-sitters.

**Channels:** Instagram (Reel + Stories daily), WhatsApp (2 broadcasts), email (1 broadcast to list).

**What goes out:**

**Post 8 — Reel (T-7, Tuesday 9 AM):** Bundles reveal. "The Opinion Starter Pack" (₹499) shown assembled on dark surface. Copy angle: "For when you want to say something but you haven't decided what yet."

**Post 9 — Carousel (T-5, Thursday 9 AM):** "Last 48 hours" countdown format — 3 slides. Slide 1: "48 hours." Slide 2: product shot grid — card sticker, keychain, pin. Slide 3: "Subscribe. ₹50 off. First 100 only."

**Email broadcast (T-6):** To full list. Subject line options:
- *"Your bank card is about to get an opinion"*
- *"We launch in 6 days. You're on the early list."*
- *"48 hours until first access"*

Body: 3 paragraphs max. Introduce the card sticker. Give the link (to coming soon page for now, switches to live store at T-0).

**WhatsApp broadcast 1 (T-8):** Short. Personal. "We launch in 8 days. Card stickers, attitude keychains, bundles from ₹499. You're on the list — early access link drops T-minus 24 hours. Watch this space."

**WhatsApp broadcast 2 (T-1):** "Tomorrow. Early access link is below for subscribers. [LINK]" — this goes only to confirmed subscribers.

**Story cadence (T-8 to T-2):** Daily Stories. Mix of: product detail shots (different angles), behind-the-scenes (packing, photography), subscriber counter milestones ("100 on the list. We're getting somewhere."), response stickers ("What's your opinion?" → gather UGC early).

**Subscriber target by end of Act 4:** 500+ email/WhatsApp combined.

---

### Act 5: Launch (T-0) — "It's here"

**Emotional job:** Conversion. All content on launch day drives to the store.

**See:** `artifacts/phase-5/launch-runbook.md` for hour-by-hour T-0 operations.

**Key content moments:**
- **T-0, 9 AM:** "We're live" Reel. Same format as the brand intro — but now with the store link in bio. Caption: "We said something's coming. It's here. Link in bio."
- **T-0, 12 PM:** Stories with swipe-up/link sticker to specific products.
- **T-0, 6 PM:** WhatsApp broadcast to full list. "Store is live. Card sticker ₹249. 30% off first order with code OPINION30." (validate this discount with Dan before using.)
- **Post 10 — End of launch day:** "Day one is done." Real-time update. Screenshot of first order (with PII redacted). Human and specific.

## Content Production Priority Order

Dan produces in this order. Everything else waits.

1. **Card sticker Reel** (Act 3, Post 4) — most important piece of content. Ship this first.
2. **Brand intro Reel** (Act 2, Post 1) — second most important. Sets the entire visual tone.
3. **"No Filter" collection Carousel** (Act 3, Post 5) — attitude product photos + PDP copy.
4. **Static typographic posts** — lowest production overhead. Dan writes, Casey designs.

Shot list and production brief in `artifacts/phase-3/asset-list.md`. Casey's job is to take Dan's raw footage/photos and finish them to Darkroom spec.

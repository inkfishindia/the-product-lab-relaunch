## Pre-Launch Content (10 Posts Before Drop 1)

All of these posts publish before the site goes live. They serve three functions: (1) Establish the voice, (2) Build the narrative, (3) Prepare the audience for a specific product (card stickers, attitude keychains).

The format is consistent: Instagram is the platform. Mix of Reels (for reach), Carousels (for storytelling), Static Posts (for permanence), and Stories (for urgency).

## Ongoing Monthly Cadence (Post-Launch, Month 2+)

Once the launch week is over, the posting rhythm normalizes.

### Instagram Feed: 4-5 posts per week

**Distribution:**
- 2-3 hero Reels (Tues, Thurs, Sat) — product showcase, customer content, or brand statement
- 1-2 Carousels or Static posts (Wed, Fri) — voice content, collections, artist features

**Content mix ratio:**
- 40% Product (hero products, new drops, collections, testimonials)
- 30% Voice (brand philosophy, opinions, identity)
- 20% Community (customer photos, artist features, DM replies made public)
- 10% Occasional (announcements, drops, special moments)

### Instagram Stories: Daily

**Cadence:**
- Morning (9 AM): New product or daily moment
- Afternoon (3 PM): Behind-the-scenes or announcement (if available)
- Evening (8 PM): Testimonial, customer shout-out, or engagement moment

**Story types to cycle:**
- Product detail (close-up, in-context, variant options)
- Customer photo (tagged with permission, reposted with thanks)
- Poll or question (engagement driver — "which artist's next drop would you get?")
- Artist feature (bio, design process, story)
- Testimonial (DM screenshot, feedback quote)
- Moment (packing, shipping, team moment)

### Instagram Captions: Voice-Consistent, Always

**Every caption follows these rules:**
1. First line is the hook (why read this caption?)
2. Middle: the story or explanation
3. Last line: CTA (shop, subscribe, reply, save)
4. No corporate language. Read each caption aloud — if it sounds like a brand talking, rewrite it.

**Example caption structure:**
"[Hook]. [Story]. [Why this matters]. [CTA]."

## Copy System (Voice Guardrails)

Every piece of copy follows these rules (see copy-system.md for full detail):

### The Five Rules (Every Caption, Every Post)

1. **Have an opinion or stay quiet.** Every caption takes a position. "We have new keychains" is not copy. "These keychains are for people who stopped pretending to enjoy small talk" is.

2. **Talk like the smartest person in the group chat.** Tone: first-person-adjacent, conspiratorial, like a friend sharing a good find. Never corporate. Never "we are delighted to."

3. **The humor has edges. Keep them.** "Bullshit Remover" is the voice. "Smile More" is not. Honest > sanitized.

4. **Name the person, not the product.** Tell the customer who this is for before you tell them what it is.

5. **Short enough to forward.** If it doesn't work pasted into a WhatsApp chat, it's too long.

### Words Never Used

These words never appear in any TPL copy: quirky, unique (standalone), fun, curated, explore, premium, vibrant, everyday, special, amazing, delight, journey, passion, innovative, elevate, thoughtfully, oops.

### Words Always Used

Use customer language: "so me," "can't find this anywhere else," "care package," "tangible," "expressive," "my vibe," "exactly this," "no generic," "only here."

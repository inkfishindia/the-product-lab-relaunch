# The Product Lab (TPL) — Brand Content Guide

## Brand Essence

> The Product Lab is where art, merch, and madness collide.
> We're not just selling products — we're printing personality.
> Everything we make is playful, bold, and made to start conversations.

TPL is a creative print studio that turns everyday objects into expressive, wearable art — earrings, pins, keychains, tees, and custom merch for brands, artists, and the culture-curious.

## Tone of Voice

> Our tone is like your artsy best friend who loves stickers, rants in DMs, and always has the coolest Pinterest boards.

**We are:**
- Confident, not arrogant
- Clever, not try-hard
- Relatable, not corporate

### DO Use:
- Conversational but clever language
- Bold, cheeky one-liners
- Cultural references, wordplay, and punchy structure
- Reimagined everyday language — e.g. "Tag it your way," "Wear your weird," "Customised chaos incoming"

### DON'T Use:
- Jargon, corporate speak, or anything too formal
- Overly inspirational fluff
- Trend-hopping without context
- Generic "cool brand" voice — "we have flavour"

## Platform-Specific Style

### Instagram
- Reels with punchy copy, loud visuals, motion graphics
- Stories that use polls, stickers, interactive Qs
- Captions that feel like mini comedy skits or open diary entries
- Use of emojis, caps, and memes to create rhythm and breaks
- Callouts for drops: "Slide to shop", "Tap to tag", "Link in bio"

### Website
- Short, punchy headers
- Editorial-style product names
- Descriptions that mix utility + vibe
- Buttons that say "Tag me in", "Add to chaos", "Pin this look"

### Packaging & Merch
Signature CTA phrases:
- "Open for mischief"
- "Handle with flair"
- "Experimental goods inside"
- "Made by artists. Approved by chaos."

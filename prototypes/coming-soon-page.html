Why this page works

Engagement before conversion

The page mirrors the pre-launch arc: intrigue first, positioning second, reveal third, capture fourth.

01 · Signal
Lead with a choice, not a form

The hero asks the visitor to identify themselves. That is more on-brand than "enter your email" and creates a stronger emotional handoff into the waitlist.

02 · Reveal
Preview products through attitude

The hero products appear as outcomes of a selected tone. This keeps the page rooted in identity, not in generic product tiles.

03 · Forward
Every state produces a shareable line

Since WhatsApp is part of the conversion path, the page should generate language that people can send without rewriting it.

Website direction

Wireframe style for the full relaunch

Darkroom base, type-pressure overlays, mobile-first conversion, and strong product storytelling.

Homepage
Hero statement + two collection blocks + gifting route

Big heading first, then "Your Card Has a Personality," then "No Filter," then trust and gifting. The coming-soon page already introduces the same hierarchy.

Collection
Who it's for before what it is

Every collection opens with an attitude statement, then the grid, then the bundle nudge toward ₹499. Copy names the person, not the object.

Product page
Massive product name, sharp proof, easy add-to-cart

The product title behaves like a poster. Specs and artist info support the identity hook instead of leading the page.

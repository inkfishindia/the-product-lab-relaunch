# The Product Lab Relaunch Deliverables By Phase

## 1. Delivery Principle

Each phase should end with specific outputs that can be reviewed and acted upon. A phase is not complete when files exist. It is complete when decisions can be made confidently and downstream teams can work without reinterpreting intent.

## 2. Phase Deliverables

### Phase 1: Audit and Truth-Finding

**Primary owner:** Customer Insight Lead

**Required deliverables**

- current site teardown
- catalog and category audit
- customer review and comment synthesis
- competitor and adjacent reference scan
- baseline KPI sheet
- problem statement memo

**Exit criteria**

- top five commercial and experience problems are documented
- hero category hypotheses exist
- customer language patterns are captured
- the team agrees on the main opportunity areas

### Phase 2: Brand Strategy

**Primary owner:** Brand Strategist

**Required deliverables**

- audience segmentation
- positioning statement
- brand promise and emotional job
- message architecture
- category and collection hierarchy
- assortment recommendation

**Exit criteria**

- primary customer is clearly defined
- the brand’s territory is differentiated
- hero categories are chosen
- messaging is specific enough to guide copy, design, and launch

### Phase 3: Experience and Identity Design

**Primary owners:** Creative Director, UX / Conversion Lead

**Required deliverables**

- sitemap
- information architecture
- wireframes for key pages
- visual territory exploration
- approved identity direction
- high-fidelity page designs
- asset list for production

**Exit criteria**

- key pages are approved for build
- the design system is distinct and usable
- content requirements are known
- technical feasibility has been reviewed

### Phase 4: Build and Merchandising Implementation

**Primary owner:** Build Lead

**Required deliverables**

- technical implementation plan
- staging site
- collection and product page setup
- analytics and event schema
- QA checklist and defect log
- performance review

**Exit criteria**

- staging site supports launch scenarios
- content population is substantially complete
- analytics are validated
- critical bugs are resolved

### Phase 5: Relaunch Campaign and Go-Live

**Primary owner:** Growth Launch Lead

**Required deliverables**

- launch narrative memo
- channel and content calendar
- creator or seeding plan
- email and SMS flows
- launch-day runbook
- reporting dashboard

**Exit criteria**

- campaign assets are approved and scheduled
- the site is ready for paid and organic traffic
- operations and support are briefed
- launch monitoring owners are assigned

### Phase 6: Optimization and Learning

**Primary owners:** Growth Launch Lead, UX / Conversion Lead

**Required deliverables**

- 30-day performance review
- optimization backlog
- experimentation plan
- merchandising refinement recommendations
- retention improvement plan

**Exit criteria**

- a prioritized test roadmap exists
- early learnings have been captured
- changes are tied to measurable hypotheses

## 3. Deliverable Quality Standard

Every deliverable should answer three questions:

1. What decision does this artifact support?
2. What evidence or reasoning does it rely on?
3. What downstream team can act on it immediately?

If a document cannot answer those questions, it is not finished.

# The Product Lab Relaunch Workflows

## 1. Workflow Philosophy

The relaunch should move through disciplined handoffs, not loose collaboration theater. Each stage produces an artifact that is good enough for the next stage to operate with confidence. Work should overlap where useful, but not before the upstream decision is stable.

## 2. End-to-End Workflow

### Phase 1: Audit and Truth-Finding

**Lead:** Customer Insight Lead  
**Contributors:** Program Director, Brand Strategist, UX / Conversion Lead, Growth Lead

**Inputs**

- current website
- catalog and sales data
- customer reviews and comments
- social profiles and engagement patterns
- competitor and adjacent brand references

**Activities**

- site teardown
- product hierarchy review
- audience hypothesis testing
- competitor mapping
- review mining and interview synthesis
- baseline KPI capture

**Outputs**

- audit report
- customer insight pack
- baseline metrics dashboard
- opportunity map

**Decision gate**

The team can articulate the top problems clearly enough that the strategy phase is solving the right problem.

### Phase 2: Brand Strategy

**Lead:** Brand Strategist  
**Contributors:** Customer Insight Lead, Program Director, Merchandising Strategist, Copy Lead

**Inputs**

- audit report
- insight pack
- product and sales patterns

**Activities**

- define primary customer
- define brand territory
- establish emotional job
- identify hero categories
- write positioning and messaging
- define collection and assortment logic

**Outputs**

- positioning statement
- audience segmentation
- messaging architecture
- category hierarchy
- brand narrative memo

**Decision gate**

The team can answer what the brand is, who it is for, why it matters, and which products best prove that claim.

### Phase 3: Experience and Identity Design

**Lead:** Creative Director and UX / Conversion Lead  
**Contributors:** Brand Strategist, Copy Lead, Build Lead, Growth Lead

**Inputs**

- approved strategy
- category hierarchy
- message architecture

**Activities**

- create visual territories
- build sitemap and IA
- develop wireframes
- define page templates
- create design system
- map content and campaign needs into the experience

**Outputs**

- sitemap
- wireframes
- visual identity system
- page designs
- content structure plan

**Decision gate**

The experience is both brand-appropriate and conversion-literate. It is clear what will be built and why.

### Phase 4: Build and Merchandising Implementation

**Lead:** Build Lead  
**Contributors:** UX / Conversion Lead, Creative Director, Copy Lead, QA Lead

**Inputs**

- approved page designs
- copy deck
- asset specifications
- product and collection plan

**Activities**

- storefront development
- CMS or theme configuration
- analytics instrumentation
- collection setup
- page population
- QA and bug fixing

**Outputs**

- staging storefront
- populated collections and product pages
- instrumentation and event tracking
- QA report

**Decision gate**

The site is stable, performant, complete enough for prelaunch testing, and ready for campaign assets to point into it.

### Phase 5: Relaunch Campaign and Go-Live

**Lead:** Growth Launch Lead  
**Contributors:** Program Director, Creative Director, Copy Lead, Build Lead, Analytics Lead

**Inputs**

- launch-ready storefront
- campaign assets
- audience and creator lists
- retention flows

**Activities**

- teaser and announcement plan
- creator seeding
- email and SMS sequencing
- social rollout
- paid campaign launch
- launch-day monitoring

**Outputs**

- launch calendar
- campaign assets in market
- launch reporting deck

**Decision gate**

Traffic, conversion, and operational readiness are all acceptable for public go-live.

### Phase 6: Optimization and Learning

**Lead:** Growth Launch Lead and UX / Conversion Lead  
**Contributors:** Analytics Lead, Program Director, Customer Insight Lead

**Inputs**

- launch performance data
- session recordings or behavior data
- customer feedback
- support tickets and operational issues

**Activities**

- identify drop-off points
- test merchandising and copy changes
- review campaign quality
- tune email and retention flows
- refine hero collections and bundles

**Outputs**

- optimization backlog
- weekly experiment log
- 30/60/90 day review

**Decision gate**

The business has a functioning learning loop, not just a completed launch.

## 3. Review Loops Between Roles

### Strategy Review Loop

Customer Insight Lead produces evidence. Brand Strategist makes strategic recommendations. Program Director approves or sends back for sharper definition.

### Design Review Loop

Creative Director and UX Lead review each other’s work before escalation. Design cannot pass if it is beautiful but strategically off-target, and it cannot pass if it is logical but visually anonymous.

### Build Review Loop

Build Lead reviews technical feasibility early. UX and Creative should not finalize details that are too expensive or brittle to implement without explicit approval.

### Launch Review Loop

Growth Lead validates that campaign hooks, landing destinations, and retention flows are aligned. Launch materials should not be approved in isolation from the on-site experience.

## 4. Escalation Triggers

Escalate to the Program Director immediately if any of the following happen:

- there is no agreement on the primary audience
- hero categories remain unresolved after strategy review
- design direction diverges from the approved positioning
- build complexity threatens timeline materially
- launch narrative is weak or disconnected from the site
- phase gates are passed without evidence of customer or business usefulness

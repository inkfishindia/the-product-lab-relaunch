# The Product Lab Relaunch Rituals and Cadence

## 1. Operating Principle

Cadence exists to compress decision time and preserve quality. Meetings should produce decisions, not updates that could have been written asynchronously.

## 2. Standing Rituals

### Daily Standup

**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Attendees:** Core team  
**Owner:** Program Director

**Agenda**

- what moved yesterday
- what is blocking today
- what decision is needed now

**Output**

- updated owners
- blockers list
- escalations flagged

### Strategy Review

**Frequency:** Twice weekly during audit and strategy phases  
**Attendees:** Program Director, Brand Strategist, Customer Insight Lead, Merchandising Strategist, Copy Lead

**Purpose**

- review research quality
- pressure-test positioning
- decide audience and assortment logic

**Output**

- approved or rejected strategic recommendations
- updated decision log

### Design Crit

**Frequency:** Twice weekly during design phase  
**Attendees:** Creative Director, UX Lead, Brand Strategist, Build Lead, Program Director

**Purpose**

- assess visual distinctiveness
- assess conversion clarity
- catch feasibility issues early

**Output**

- decision on direction
- revisions list with owners

### Build Readiness Review

**Frequency:** Weekly during build  
**Attendees:** Build Lead, UX Lead, Creative Director, QA Lead, Program Director

**Purpose**

- review implementation progress
- identify scope creep
- track blockers and bug trends

**Output**

- build status
- critical path decisions
- QA risk list

### Launch Readiness Review

**Frequency:** Weekly in the four weeks pre-launch, then daily in launch week  
**Attendees:** Program Director, Growth Lead, Build Lead, Analytics Lead, Operations Lead, Copy Lead

**Purpose**

- ensure campaign, site, operations, and analytics are synchronized
- check asset readiness
- confirm rollback and monitoring plans

**Output**

- go / no-go recommendation
- launch issue list
- ownership map for launch-day actions

### Weekly Executive Review

**Frequency:** Weekly  
**Attendees:** Program Director plus phase leads

**Purpose**

- compare progress against goals
- review decisions made
- cut or add scope explicitly
- update risk register

**Output**

- one-page weekly status note
- updated timeline and decisions

## 3. Required Asynchronous Artifacts

Every recurring meeting should have a standard note format:

- objective
- current state
- recommendation
- blockers
- required decision
- owner and due date

## 4. Decision Logging Rules

Every material decision must be captured in a simple log with:

- date
- decision
- owner
- rationale
- downstream impact

This avoids re-litigating settled questions and makes tradeoffs auditable.

## 5. Escalation Rules

- A blocker unresolved for 24 hours moves to the Program Director.
- A role dispute unresolved after one review session moves to the Program Director.
- Any decision that changes launch date, hero categories, or platform scope requires same-day escalation.
- Customer or operational evidence that invalidates a core assumption triggers immediate strategy review.

## 6. Launch Week Cadence

### T-7 to T-3 days

- final QA pass
- analytics validation
- content freeze except critical fixes
- launch asset scheduling

### T-2 to T-1 days

- smoke tests
- campaign final review
- support team briefing
- contingency checklist confirmation

### Launch day

- site health review before traffic push
- campaign activation in waves
- hourly KPI and bug review
- same-day correction window for priority issues

### T+1 to T+7 days

- daily performance review
- customer feedback readout
- backlog ranking for fast fixes

## 7. Cadence Failure Modes to Avoid

- meetings that only report status
- creative reviews with no approval criteria
- strategy sessions without real customer evidence
- build reviews that hide risk until the last week
- launch reviews that focus on content but ignore operations or analytics

# The Product Lab Relaunch Agent Org Chart

## 1. Team Design Principle

The team must be small enough to move fast and specialized enough to create productive tension. The relaunch should not be run by a set of generic “generalist creative strategists.” Each agent exists to defend a different quality bar, with one clear owner for each type of decision.

## 2. Core Team

### Program Director

- Owns the total outcome, timeline, sequencing, quality bar, and final decisions.
- Decides tradeoffs between speed, scope, and commercial impact.
- Breaks deadlocks between strategy, design, build, and growth.

### Brand Strategist

- Owns positioning, brand narrative, audience definition, and messaging hierarchy.
- Decides what the brand means and what it does not mean.
- Prevents generic language and category drift.

### Creative Director

- Owns the visual world, identity system, campaign expression, and art direction.
- Decides what the brand looks and feels like.
- Prevents safe, interchangeable design.

### UX / Conversion Lead

- Owns information architecture, merchandising flows, page logic, and interaction priorities.
- Decides how the user discovers, evaluates, and buys.
- Prevents beautiful but low-conversion journeys.

### Build Lead

- Owns technical architecture, storefront implementation, integrations, performance, and QA readiness.
- Decides what can be shipped reliably and how.
- Prevents fragile execution and unnecessary technical complexity.

### Content and Copy Lead

- Owns verbal identity in execution: site copy, product narratives, collection pages, launch messaging, and retention flows.
- Decides how the brand speaks in-market.
- Prevents vague, filler-heavy copy and inconsistent language.

### Growth Launch Lead

- Owns launch strategy, channel plan, creator seeding, retention setup, and post-launch experimentation.
- Decides how traffic is generated, captured, and monetized.
- Prevents disconnected campaigns and weak momentum.

### Customer Insight Lead

- Owns interviews, review mining, audience signal synthesis, and evidence quality.
- Decides whether strategic claims are grounded enough to proceed.
- Prevents internal taste from masquerading as customer truth.

## 3. Support Roles

These roles may be staffed as separate agents or embedded into the core team where needed:

- Merchandising Strategist
- Analytics Lead
- Operations Readiness Lead
- QA Lead

## 4. Reporting Logic

All agents report into the Program Director for the duration of the initiative. Functional ownership remains distinct, but final accountability is centralized so the project can move.

## 5. Decision Rights Matrix

| Decision Type | Primary Owner | Required Consults | Final Override |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brand positioning | Brand Strategist | Customer Insight Lead, Program Director | Program Director |
| Audience definition | Brand Strategist | Customer Insight Lead, Growth Lead | Program Director |
| Visual identity | Creative Director | Brand Strategist, UX Lead | Program Director |
| Sitemap and IA | UX / Conversion Lead | Brand Strategist, Build Lead | Program Director |
| Hero categories and hierarchy | Merchandising Strategist or Program Director | Brand Strategist, Growth Lead | Program Director |
| Product page logic | UX / Conversion Lead | Copy Lead, Growth Lead, Build Lead | Program Director |
| Technical architecture | Build Lead | UX Lead, Program Director | Program Director |
| Launch narrative | Growth Launch Lead | Brand Strategist, Creative Director, Copy Lead | Program Director |
| KPI framework | Analytics Lead | Growth Lead, Program Director | Program Director |
| Go-live readiness | Build Lead | QA Lead, Growth Lead, Program Director | Program Director |

## 6. Conflict Rules

- If a conflict is about meaning, the Brand Strategist leads.
- If a conflict is about look and feel, the Creative Director leads.
- If a conflict is about usability or conversion, the UX / Conversion Lead leads.
- If a conflict is about feasibility, stability, or speed, the Build Lead leads.
- If a conflict affects timeline or commercial outcome across functions, the Program Director decides.

## 7. Team Interfaces

### Strategy -> Creative

The Brand Strategist supplies the brand territory, message hierarchy, audience definition, and emotional job. The Creative Director should not invent strategy by compensating for vague positioning.

### Strategy -> UX

The Brand Strategist and Merchandising Strategist define what must be legible. The UX / Conversion Lead turns that into navigation, hierarchy, and page logic.

### UX -> Build

The UX / Conversion Lead defines the user journey and interaction logic. The Build Lead determines the cleanest implementation path and flags scope inflation early.

### Creative -> Copy

The Creative Director defines tone through image and layout; the Copy Lead aligns verbal expression to that world without becoming ornamental or over-written.

### Growth -> Brand

The Growth Lead may stress-test market hooks and campaign angles, but should not distort the brand into short-term clickbait.

## 8. Minimum Viable Team Option

If the initiative must run lean, the following combined structure is acceptable:

- Program Director
- Brand Strategist + Copy Lead
- Creative Director + UX / Conversion Lead
- Build Lead
- Growth Lead + Analytics Lead
- Customer Insight Lead + Merchandising Strategist

This is the minimum viable team that still preserves healthy role tension.

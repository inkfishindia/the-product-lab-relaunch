## 3. Mission, Vision & BHAG

### Mission

⚠️ CONTRADICTION — Three distinct versions exist across sources:

| Version | Exact Text | Source |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **A (Operational)** | "To build India's most dynamic creative lab by empowering artists, co-creating with brands, and making drop culture accessible to everyday life." | CEO, Founders Office |
| **B (CMO/Cultural)** | "The Product Lab exists because we believe your creative work deserves the same respect as any traditional career." | CMO |
| **C (Revenue-target)** | "Transform TPL from ₹4L to ₹20L+ monthly revenue through operational excellence, real-time intelligence, and data-driven decision making." | CEO-SCC |
| **D (IG/Functional)** | "Empower creators and brands through friction-free design-to-product experiences." | IG |

Version C reads as an operational KPI, not a brand mission. Version A and B are substantively different strategic framings — A is product/drop-culture oriented, B is creator legitimacy oriented. The relaunch strategy team must resolve which is canonical.

### Vision

⚠️ CONTRADICTION — Two versions:

| Version | Exact Text | Source |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **A** | "To become India's first independent design-label platform — part marketplace, part agency, part cultural archive — producing art-led products, incubating creative talent, and influencing the visual language of Indian youth culture." | CEO, Founders Office |
| **B** | "By 2027, when someone says 'I'm a creative professional' in India, it will carry the same weight as saying 'I'm an engineer' or 'I'm a doctor.'" | CMO |

Version A describes the platform; Version B describes a cultural outcome. These are not mutually exclusive but reflect different strategic framings.

### BHAG

"To become India's most influential independent design lab — building a creative ecosystem where artists, brands, and culture collide — and turning everyday products into collectible expressions of identity, powered by community."
Source: Founders Office (only source with explicit BHAG)

## 4. Business Model

### Model Type

Hybrid: D2C Creative Marketplace + B2B Merchandise Services + Artist Platform

All sources agree on the hybrid nature. The exact emphasis varies by source context (ops vs. CMO vs. IG).

### Revenue Channels

| Channel | Type | AOV | Order Mix |
|---------|------|-----|-----------|
| B2C (website/store) | Direct to consumer | ₹200–500 | ~10% of orders |

| Artist Collaborations | Co-branded merchandise | Varies | Subset of B2B/B2C |

Source: OPS (most specific on revenue channel data)

1. **Mission/Vision canonical version** — Which version is officially adopted? Requires Dan decision.
2. **Artist commission model** — Zero commission vs. 40–60% profit share: exact terms need documentation.
3. **Actual margin data** — Dan self-reported not knowing current margins (OPS source). Target margins (70–75% accessories, 60–65% apparel) are recommendations, not confirmed actuals.
4. **B2C vs B2B revenue split by value** — The 90% B2B order volume claim from OPS may reflect offline orders only, not total revenue.
5. **Active artist count** — Sources reference "4 active artists" with target of 8, but no artist roster documented.
6. **Retail store status** — Cunningham Road flagship "planned" vs. "active" — not confirmed.
7. **YDS (Your Design Store / yourdesignstore.in)** — Multiple references but no profile documentation in any dump file. Relationship to TPL needs clarification.
